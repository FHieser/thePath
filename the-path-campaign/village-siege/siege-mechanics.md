t# Village Siege Mechanics

A framework for running siege encounters on the Village in the Mist.

---

## Siege Trigger

The siege begins when the GM's Fear reaches a threshold (recommended: 10+ Fear). The Sacred Flame **flares** - a desperate pulse of divine energy that draws the mist's attention. The Mist Born respond with overwhelming force.

---

## Zone Layout

The battlefield consists of three concentric rings surrounding the Village Center.

### Mist Wall Ring (Outermost)
Four zones (North, East, South, West) at the edge of the Sacred Flame's influence, deep in the mist. This is where siege engines stage and reinforcements spawn. **Always Mist-Touched.**

### Outer Ring (Middle)
Four zones (North, East, South, West) forming the first defensive perimeter. **Protected by the Sacred Flame unless an anchor is present.**

### Inner Ring (Innermost)
Four zones (North, East, South, West) forming the last line of defense before the village proper. **Protected by the Sacred Flame unless an anchor is present.**

### Village Center
The Sacred Flame and village infrastructure. Not a positioning zone for NPCs.

**If enemies reach the Village Center:** Each round, the Sacred Flame loses 1 Flame Essence and all PCs gain 1 Mist token. If all Essence is lost, the village falls.

### Zone Connections
- Each Mist Wall zone connects to its corresponding Outer zone
- Each Outer zone connects to its corresponding Inner zone
- Each ring's zones connect to adjacent zones in that ring (North↔East↔South↔West)
- All Inner zones connect to Village Center

Enemies must move through zones sequentially.

---

## Turn Structure

Each round consists of two phases:

1. **GM Phase**: All enemies activate one at a time. Each enemy gets 1 action (usually move 1 zone).
2. **Player Phase**: All players act.

**Combat Engagement Rule**: If enemies share a zone with NPCs or players, they MUST use their activation to fight. They cannot move past defenders.

---

## Starting Forces

### Enemy Budget
**Total Battle Points = 9 × (number of PCs) + 6**

| Party Size | Total BP |
|------------|----------|
| 2 PCs | 24 BP |
| 3 PCs | 33 BP |
| 4 PCs | 42 BP |
| 5 PCs | 51 BP |

The Siege Engine costs **10 BP**. The GM distributes remaining BP as enemies across Mist-Touched zones.

### Enemy Placement Constraint
Enemies can ONLY exist in Mist-Touched zones:
- **Mist Wall Ring**: Always Mist-Touched
- **Outer Ring**: Only Mist-Touched if anchor present or Mist Thrower uses "Spread the Mist"
- **Inner Ring**: Only Mist-Touched if anchor present or Mist Thrower uses "Spread the Mist"

---

## Fear Economy

### Fear Generation (Start of Each Round)
| Source | Fear Generated |
|--------|----------------|
| Active Mist Anchor | +1 |
| Active Siege Engine | +2 |

### Fear Spending: Summon Reinforcements
Reinforcements spawn in Mist Wall Ring only.

| Fear Spent | BP Gained |
|------------|-----------|
| 2 | 2 |
| 3 | 4 |
| 4 | 6 |
| 5 | 9 |
| 6 | 12 |
| 7 | 15 |
| 8 | 20 |

### Siege End Condition
The siege ends when the GM has **0 Fear AND no active anchors AND no active siege engines**.

---

## Phases

### Phase 1: Preparation
**Trigger**: Siege announced
**Ends**: First enemy activation

Players position themselves and NPCs. NPC deployment is instant within their allowed zones.

### Phase 2: Open Fields
**Trigger**: First enemy activation
**Ends**: Siege Engine reaches Inner Ring

Combat in Mist Wall and Outer rings. Siege engine advances, deploys anchors.

### Phase 3: Inner Siege
**Trigger**: Siege Engine reaches Inner Ring
**Ends**: Victory or defeat

Desperate defense of the inner perimeter and Village Center.

---

## Victory Conditions

### Total Defense
- Destroy all Mist Anchors
- Destroy all Siege Engines
- GM Fear reaches 0 with no generation remaining

### Tactical Retreat
- Evacuate key NPCs and Sacred Flame essence
- Accept village may be lost

---

## Loss Conditions

### Village Falls
Sacred Flame loses all Essence.
- Protective barrier collapses
- All PCs gain 3 Mist tokens
- Village becomes Mist-Touched ruin
