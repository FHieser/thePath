# The Watcher

**Faction:** The Rootbound
**Location:** Forest Refuge
**Role:** Guardian of the fairy lights and mushroom ring

---

## Appearance
- Wears a deer skull with antlers as a mask/head
- Robe woven entirely from living moss
- Moves silently through the trees

## Personality
- Cannot speak - communicates through gestures and body language
- Judges visitors through observation
- Protective of the sanctuary but not hostile unless provoked

## The Twist
**Silent Guardian:** The Watcher cannot speak at all. All communication happens through gestures, pointing, and the environment responding to their presence (fairy lights brightening/dimming, forest sounds changing). Players must interpret their intentions.

## Theme in Relation to PCs
**Respect vs. Exploitation** - The Watcher judges whether PCs treat sanctuaries as sacred spaces or resources to be exploited. Mirrors the party's relationship with nature and ancient places. Rewards reverence, punishes greed. Forces PCs to prove their intentions through actions, not words.

## Goals & Motivations
- Protect Forest Refuge's natural mist resistance
- Test if visitors respect the sanctuary
- Ensure the fairy lights and mushroom ring remain safe

## What They Offer
- Safe rest in Forest Refuge (if visitors prove respectful)
- Herbal medicine and tea
- Health potion (as gift after players help)
- Access to mushroom ring teleportation (points to it)

## What They Want
- Proof visitors respect nature (don't harm fairy lights, mushroom ring, or forest)
- Visitors to leave the sanctuary as they found it

## Key Interaction Beats
- **Hidden Observer:** The Watcher hides in the forest while Factionless marauders occupy the dwelling
- **After Combat:** Once players deal with the marauders, The Watcher emerges from the trees
- **Grateful Guardian:** Gestures thanks, offers tea and a health potion as gift
- **The Offer:** Invites players to rest safely, points to mushroom ring and fairy lights
- **Departure:** Points toward the Path ahead (warning gesture), fades back into forest
