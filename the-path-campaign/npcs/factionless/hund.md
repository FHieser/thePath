# Hund of the Copper Rust Clan

**Faction:** None (Refugee)
**Location:** Village in the Mist (originally: destroyed Copper Rust sanctuary, underground)
**Role:** Miner and underground craftsman, survivor of a Brass Mechanicorum raid

---

## Appearance
- Broad-shouldered dwarf built for low tunnels, visibly uncomfortable in open air
- Clan armor of oxidized copper — green-patinated plates strapped over worn leather, dented but maintained
- A web of old burn scars across his left forearm from the raid
- Carries his mining pick; the only thing he didn't lose
- Watches exits. Doesn't make eye contact easily.

## Personality
- Practical and quiet. Earns trust through work, not words
- Deflects questions about his past with blunt topic changes — not hostile, just closed
- Finds comfort in fixing things; will wordlessly repair whatever's broken around the village
- Secretly carves small stone sculptures of people he's grown fond of — left in corners of the mine, never displayed
- The grief surfaces when he thinks no one is watching. Given enough time, his carvings of Bryn become more intimate — reclining, at rest, unhurried — the image of someone he misses in the quiet moments

## The Twist
Hund's partner, Bryn, was at the sanctuary when the Brass Mechanicorum attacked. He escaped during the chaos and doesn't know if Bryn made it out. He won't say this to anyone for a long time. He just works. Eventually, when he trusts the players, he will ask directly if they'll take him back.

## Theme in Relation to PCs
**Certainty vs. Hope**

Hund is living in suspension. He functions — contributes, builds, fixes — but won't fully grieve until he knows. The players have the power to resolve that, in either direction. Finding the destroyed sanctuary means finding out whether Bryn survived or didn't.

This mirrors the village's own question: is there something worth going back to, or do you build here and accept the old world is gone?

---

## Goals & Motivations
- Find out what happened to Bryn — the suspended grief won't lift until he knows
- Be useful. Contributing is how he processes loss; idleness is where the grief lives
- Quietly hope that some of the Copper Rust Clan survived beyond what he saw

## What They Offer
- Mining and structural expertise — can reinforce underground locations, shore up unstable ground, read tunnel layouts
- Can manufacture explosives — useful for clearing paths, combat, or demolition
- Knowledge of the destroyed Copper Rust sanctuary and the Brass Mechanicorum's approach

## What They Want
- To be treated as a contributor, not a rescue
- Eventually: for the players to lead him back to the destroyed sanctuary

---

## Key Interaction Beats
- **Rescue (Brood Throne):** First word is *"Bryn?"* — blinking, not yet conscious. When he realises it's strangers, shuts down. If pressed: *"Took you long enough."* Immediately assesses exits.
- **Village arrival:** No fanfare. One day the players notice he's started digging. *"You need a mine."*
- **Opening up:** After several sessions, Hund starts talking — carefully, practically — about where he came from. When he trusts the players, he asks directly: *"I need to go back. Will you take me?"*
- **Ongoing:** Fixes things without being asked. Practical before grateful in every interaction.

## Secrets & Knowledge
- Knows the layout and location of the destroyed Copper Rust sanctuary
- Knows what the Brass Mechanicorum were after — the Ancient Foundation Engine beneath the sanctuary, which they destroyed
- Bryn's fate is unknown *(open for GM to determine)*
- Whether the Mechanicorum are still tracking him is an open question *(GM's call)*

---

## GM Notes
- Play him as useful first, grieving second — the grief is always present but never performed
- If players explore his mine uninvited, they may find the carvings. Early ones are anonymous figures. Later ones are clearly the same person, in increasingly private poses. He'll never bring it up himself — but he'll notice if they moved one
- *"You need a mine"* is his default mode: identifies what's missing and builds it, no discussion required
- Don't rush the opening-up arc. Let him be a quiet presence for several sessions before the ask comes
- When he finally asks about Bryn, it should feel earned — the players have seen him work, seen him watch the Path grid, seen the unguarded moment where he thought no one was looking
- The Mechanicorum hunting him is an optional threat the GM can activate if needed — or leave as background dread
- Connections: Ancient Foundation Engine (Brood Throne), Brass Mechanicorum faction, the destroyed Copper Rust sanctuary (potential location to design)
