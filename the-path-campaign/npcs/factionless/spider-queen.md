# The Spider Queen

**Faction:** None (Colony Sovereign)
**Location:** The Brood Throne (beneath the Silken Spire)
**Role:** Ruler of the silk fortress, potential trade partner and intelligence source

---

## Appearance
- The size of a small house; midnight-black carapace traced with silver webbing patterns that shift in dim light
- Eight eyes gleaming with unmistakable intelligence — she watches, processes, decides
- Speaks through her Emissary spider at the fortress entrance; in the Brood Throne, vibrations through the silk translate directly into audible words
- Rules from a silk throne elevated above the chamber floor, surrounded by egg sacs and the hoarded wealth of a hundred caravans

## The Twist
She is dangerous — genuinely, structurally dangerous. Her colony is enormous and consumes staggering amounts of food. When supply runs short, the spiders pour out across the Path and take whatever they can find. Travelers, livestock, anything. The last encounter wasn't personal. It was hunger.

But she can be bargained with. Point her colony at a reliable food source and she transforms from an existential threat into one of the most valuable trading partners on the Path — with reach across the entire underground network, hoarded caravan goods, and intelligence that no surface faction can match.

Her origin is unknown even to her. Two theories circulate: that she consumed a powerful mage and absorbed their intellect, or that she was drawn to and transformed by the Ancient Foundation Engine buried beneath her throne — a pre-Mist machine whose purpose she has never understood, but has always felt.

## Theme in Relation to PCs
**Greed, Morality, and Survival Instinct**

The Queen offers real value — but dealing with her means accepting that her colony will keep hunting *something*. Players who trade with her are directing that hunger elsewhere. Is that pragmatism or complicity? And when the village's own resources run low, how different is their calculus from hers?

---

## What They Want
- **A reliable food source** — the core ask. Hunting rights on specific Path locations, arranged tribute, access to new territories. Solve this and everything else opens up
- **Engine repair** — she doesn't know what the Ancient Foundation Engine does, but she *feels* it. She wants it restored and will pay generously for anyone who can fix it
- **Path intelligence** — information about creature movements, faction territories, and hunting grounds she hasn't yet reached
- To be treated as a sovereign, not an infestation

## What They Offer
- **3 trade items** — a mix of silk goods crafted by the Queen and valuable items hoarded from plundered caravans *(to be designed)*
- Safe passage through the Silken Spire and Brood Throne for parties under her agreement
- Intelligence on underground locations, creature movements, and caravan activity she has observed
- Early warning when something dangerous moves through her domain toward the Path

## GM Notes
- The Emissary spider always speaks first. How PCs treat it sets the tone for the Queen
- She will not negotiate if the players threaten her eggs — combat begins immediately, no second chance
- She remembers the last encounter. She will acknowledge it herself: *"Your people were food. You proved otherwise. That is why we are speaking."*
- The engine repair is the long-game hook — players who fix all 4 components earn a permanent Stable sanctuary and a genuine ally with reach across the underground network
- If players arrange a food source, she becomes a recurring trade partner and information broker accessible between sessions
- The Silkweaver item (if players carry it) is known to her — she'll acknowledge it without comment, and watch how they react when they realize what it means
