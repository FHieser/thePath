# Spider Queen

**Tier 2 Solo**

*An ancient arachnid the size of a small house, her midnight-black carapace adorned with silver webbing patterns that shimmer with otherworldly intelligence. Eight eyes gleam with cunning awareness as she surveys her domain from the silk throne, her presence commanding absolute obedience from every spider in the fortress.*

**Motives and Tactics:** Protect the brood, test intruders' worth, negotiate from strength, summon reinforcements, rule the colony

**Difficulty:** 15
**Thresholds:** 13/26
**HP:** 9
**Stress:** 4
**ATK:** +4 **Royal Fangs:** Close — 2d10+4 phy
**Experience:** Command +3, Insight +2, Occult +2

## Features

### Relentless (2) — Passive
The Spider Queen can be spotlighted up to two times per GM turn. Spend Fear as usual to spotlight her.

### Summon the Brood — Action
**Spend a Fear** to have the Queen screech a summoning call that echoes through the ceiling tunnels above her throne. Spiders pour from the holes—add up to 3 Battle Points worth of spider adversaries to the encounter. They appear within Close range of the Queen, emerging from the silk-wrapped tunnels that honeycomb the chamber ceiling.

### Necrotoxin Strike — Action
**Mark a Stress** to inject a devastating magical venom. Make an attack against a target within Close range. On a success, deal 3d6+6 direct physical damage and the target must mark a Stress or become Vulnerable as the toxin attacks their nervous system. The Vulnerable condition lasts until they succeed on a Strength Roll to metabolize the poison.

### Queen's Command — Passive
All spider adversaries within Far range of the Queen gain +1 to their attack rolls while she lives. Her presence emboldens the entire colony, coordinating their attacks through subtle pheromone signals and vibrations through the silk.

### Momentum — Reaction
When the Spider Queen makes a successful attack against a PC, you gain a Fear.

---

*No one knows how long she has ruled this domain, or what granted her the spark of true intelligence. Some say she was once a lesser spider who consumed a powerful mage; others whisper she was touched by ancient magic sealed beneath the earth. Whatever her origin, she is cunning, patient, and fiercely protective of her brood. The Queen can be reasoned with—she values strength and respects worthy adversaries. But threaten her eggs, and diplomacy ends in fangs and venom.*
