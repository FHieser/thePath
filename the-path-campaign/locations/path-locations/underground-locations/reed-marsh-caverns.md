# REED MARSH CAVERNS

**_Tier 1 Exploration_**
_A vast underground wetland where tall reeds grow in shallow pools, warm mist rises from vapor vents, and bioluminescent larvae create a false starry sky above._
**Impulses:** Conceal and disorient, shelter the reclusive, reveal strange beauty in darkness

> **Difficulty:** 11
> **Modifiers:** Underground, Unstable, Inhabited, Mushroom Circle
> **Potential Adversaries:** Cave Beasts (Giant Frog, Pale Salamander), Amphibious Predators, The Hermit (neutral/hostile), BFF - Big Frog (Tier 2 Solo)

## FEATURES

**_Glowworm Grotto - Passive:_** The cave ceiling is covered in bioluminescent larvae that emit a soft blue-green glow, providing dim light throughout the location. PCs can harvest glowworm clusters with an Instinct roll. On a success, they gather bioluminescent material that provides light for 24 hours. On a success with Fear, they disturb the colonies - the glow dims significantly for the next hour, reducing visibility and imposing disadvantage on Instinct rolls to navigate.

_What patterns do the glowworms create on the ceiling, and what do they resemble? What happens if someone uses fire or loud noise near the glowworm colonies? Do the glowworms react to the presence of certain creatures or magic?_

**_Reed Marsh Mists - Passive:_** Dense walls of tall reeds combine with warm mist from underground vents, creating a perpetually obscured wetland maze. The environment heavily obscures distant sight. All creatures have disadvantage on action rolls to move quickly or navigate through the reeds. Attack rolls against targets at Far or Very Far range have disadvantage.

_What landmarks can PCs use to navigate through the reed maze? How do creatures native to this location navigate when the mist is thickest? What happens when someone gets separated from their group in the reeds?_

**_The Hermit's Domain - Action:_** The PCs discover signs of habitation - a lived-in cave with crude furniture, provisions, and strange trophies. If the PCs investigate or make noise, the Hermit emerges (along with BFF if threatened). The Hermit is initially wary but not hostile. They can provide information about the caverns, trade supplies, or warn about dangers. However, if PCs steal, threaten, or harm the environment, the Hermit becomes hostile and BFF (a Tier 2 Solo giant frog) enters combat to defend their friend.

_What drove the Hermit to live alone in these caverns? What strange knowledge or secrets does the Hermit possess? How did the Hermit befriend such a massive and dangerous creature?_

**_Croaking Chorus - Action:_** **Spend a Fear** to have thousands of normal-sized frogs throughout the cavern begin croaking in unison, creating a deafening cacophony. All creatures must mark 1 Stress from the overwhelming noise. Additionally, the noise attracts attention - if BFF is not already present, you gain a Fear as the giant frog investigates the disturbance.

_What triggers the smaller frogs to respond to BFF's presence? How do the different species of frogs create their layered chorus? What other creatures are drawn to or repelled by the croaking?_

**_Mushroom Ring Magic - Passive:_** This location has the Mushroom Circle modifier.

_Where does the ring connect to this session? What strange sensations accompany the teleportation? What fey entities might notice frequent use?_

## FEATURE QUESTIONS

- What connection exists between the underground wetlands and the surface world above?
- How does the Hermit survive in this isolated and dangerous environment?
- What ancient purpose did the mushroom ring serve before the caverns flooded?
- What lurks in the deeper pools beyond where the glowworm light reaches?
