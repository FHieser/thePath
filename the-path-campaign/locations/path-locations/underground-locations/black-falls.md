# BLACK FALLS

***Tier 2 Exploration***
*A treacherous underground passage where black water cascades down multi-tiered falls, creating haunting rainbows in the mist while ancient stone penitents kneel eternally along the narrow path.*
**Impulses:** Test balance and courage, preserve ancient pilgrim routes, warn of the depths below

> **Difficulty:** 14
> **Modifiers:** Unstable, Underground
> **Potential Adversaries:** Cave Predators, Ancient Spirits, Stone Guardians, Mist Walkers, Mist Stalkers

## FEATURES

***The Shelf Road - Traversal/Action:*** A narrow ledge carved along the cavern wall serves as the only path forward. To one side, sheer rock face rises into darkness. To the other, a deadly drop plunges 60 feet to jagged rocks and black water pools below. The path is only 3-4 feet wide, slick with moisture from the falls. When PCs traverse the Shelf Road, they make an **Agility action roll**. On a **success**, navigate safely across the treacherous ledge. On a **success with Hope**, you help steady the next person - they gain advantage on their roll. On a **failure**, you slip on the wet stone and must mark a Stress as you catch yourself at the edge. On a **failure with Fear**, mark a Stress and take **2d6+4 physical damage** as you fall partway down, landing on a lower outcropping. You are separated from the group and must find another way to rejoin them.

  *How wide is the narrowest section of the Shelf Road? What caused this ledge to be carved - natural erosion or ancient hands? How far does the drop extend, and what lies at the bottom?*

***Black Falls - Passive/Hazard:*** Sheets of black water cascade down the cavern wall, stained pitch-black by ancient mineral deposits. The deafening roar makes communication difficult within Close range - PCs have disadvantage on Instinct rolls based on sound. Black water spray keeps the Shelf Road perpetually slick, and eerie rainbows form where the water catches bioluminescent light. The GM can spend **3 Fear** to trigger **Mist Corruption**: The falls suddenly run thick with supernatural mist instead of water, pouring down like a liquid fog. The location immediately becomes **Mist-Touched**. All PCs in the location must immediately mark **1 Mist Token** as the corruption washes over them. Visibility drops to Close range, mist adversaries can spawn from the falls, and the deafening roar is replaced by oppressive silence broken only by distant whispers. The corruption persists until cleansed or the PCs leave the location.

  *What creates the mineral staining? Where does the water originate? Does the black stain wash off?*

***The Watching Gallery - Passive:*** A natural stone balcony juts from the cavern wall, providing a commanding view of the cavern system. The carved stone penitent figure flanking this platform creates an atmosphere of ancient sanctity. When PCs reach the Gallery, the GM describes one Feature from a connected location that PCs can observe from this vantage point (GM chooses which location is visible based on the current Path configuration). The Gallery's spiritual atmosphere and shelter from the falls make it ideal for rest - when PCs rest here, the GM gains **1 fewer Fear** than normal (minimum 1).

  *What locations are typically visible from this vantage? Why does the penitent figure create such a calming presence? Were pilgrims meant to rest and pray here?*

***Multi-Tiered Descent - Counter System:*** The black water cascades down four distinct tiers, each about 15-20 feet tall. Wet stone ledges and slick handholds allow descent or ascent beside the flowing water. A **carved stone penitent figure** kneels at each tier landing, marking the pilgrim route. Start a **Descent Progress counter**: **If descending**, start at 4 and count down to 0. **If ascending**, start at 0 and count up to 4. Each tier, PCs make a **group Agility roll**. If at least half succeed, advance the counter by 1. On failure, all PCs mark **1 Stress** and the counter doesn't advance. On failure with Fear, all PCs mark 1 Stress, counter doesn't advance, and one PC (players choose) slips and falls to the next tier, taking **1d8+3 physical damage**. When the counter reaches destination (0 descending, 4 ascending), PCs complete the descent/ascent.

  *How treacherous is each tier compared to the others? What lies at the bottom? Can you climb back up easily?*

***The Penitent Path - Passive/Action:*** Five carved stone figures kneel along the pilgrim route - one at the Watching Gallery and one at each of the four descent tier landings. They face the falls in eternal prayer, hands raised in supplication, worn smooth by centuries of black water. These ancient monuments mark a sacred pilgrimage path. PCs who **pray at each stone penitent in sequence** (starting from the Gallery, then all four descent tiers in order) complete the sacred journey. After praying at all five figures, each PC who participated gains: **Gain 2 Hope**, **Remove 2 Stress**, or **Remove 2 Mist Tokens** (choose one). This blessing can only be received once per PC. However, PCs who don't pray at all feel the weight of the sacred place watching them and must mark **1 Stress**.

  *What religion or culture carved these monuments? What were pilgrims seeking when they made this descent? Where does the pilgrim path lead at the bottom?*

## FEATURE QUESTIONS

- What sacred site were the pilgrims traveling to when they walked this path?
- Does the black water ever run clear, or has it always been dark?
- What creatures make their home in the caverns visible from the Watching Gallery?
- How deep does the multi-tiered descent extend before reaching the underground lake?
- Is there any connection between the stone penitents and the Bleeding Altar at Black Beach?
