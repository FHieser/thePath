# FLOODED NETWORK

***Tier 1 Traversal***
*A network of water-filled tunnels and chambers where underground springs feed dark lakes and ancient machinery still turns with the current.*
**Impulses:** Channel water through hidden passages, power forgotten mechanisms, test endurance and navigation skills

> **Difficulty:** 11
> **Modifiers:** Unstable, Underground
> **Potential Adversaries:** Aquatic Predators (Giant Pike, Tunnel Eel), Water Spirits, Mechanical Guardians

## FEATURES

***Network Traversal - Counter System:*** Navigating through the flooded tunnels requires persistence and careful progress. Start a counter at 0. Each time PCs make progress through the network (successful navigation attempts, solving water puzzles, or finding shortcuts), increment the counter by 1.   *Which routes through the tunnels are safest? How do water levels affect available passages? What landmarks help navigate in the dark waters?*

***Underground Lake Crossing - Passive:*** Dark bodies of water block key passages throughout the network. When PCs encounter a lake crossing, they must choose their approach: swim directly (Strength roll), find a way around (Instinct roll), or use the machinery. Success advances the traversal counter, while failure may trigger other features or require alternate routes.

  *How deep are these underground lakes? What dangers lurk beneath the dark waters? Are there hidden stepping stones or platforms?*

***Ancient Water Wheel Control - Action:*** Massive brass and iron machinery can be operated to change water levels and open new passages. When PCs attempt to use the water wheel, make a Lore roll. On a success, advance the traversal counter and choose to either drain a flooded passage or raise water to access higher tunnels. On a failure, trigger the Hydraulic Surge feature.

  *What was this machinery originally designed to accomplish? How do different wheel positions affect the water flow? What happens if multiple wheels are operated simultaneously?*

***Hydraulic Surge - Reaction:*** When the ancient machinery malfunctions or PCs fail to navigate properly, water pressure builds dangerously. All PCs must succeed on a Strength Reaction Roll or be swept backwards (lose 1 from traversal counter, minimum 0) and mark 1 Stress from the turbulent waters. The surge also attracts aquatic predators to investigate the disturbance.

  *What causes the water pressure to build so dramatically? How quickly does the surge travel through the tunnels? Are there safe alcoves to shelter during surges?*

## FEATURE QUESTIONS

- What ancient civilization engineered this sophisticated underground water system?
- How do the water wheels connect to create a network-wide hydraulic system?
- What shortcuts or secret routes might experienced tunnel navigators know?
- Could mastering this system provide access to other underground locations?
