# Devil's Rock

***Tier 2 Social***
*A throne of stone above the endless mist, where wishes are granted and fates are sealed.*

**Impulses:** To whisper promises that sound like salvation • To stand as proof that some bargains cannot be undone • To echo with the king's final words

> **Difficulty:** 14
> **Modifiers:** Unstable, Routes(1)
> **Potential Adversaries:** The Devil

---

## Features

### The Throne Stone — Passive

A massive stone precipice jutting over the sea of mist like a throne for something that never sits. The climb up is treacherous; the view from the top is infinite nothing. The energy here is wrong—heavy with old promises and older regrets.

**Mechanics:**
- Stepping onto the Throne Stone costs **2 Hope** (automatic, no roll)
- Any effect that pushes or knocks a PC off the edge triggers **Off-Path Travel** rules—they are lost in the mist
- The stone juts over the endless sea of mist; there are no guardrails, no safety

*GM Question: What desperation drove this character to seek Devil's Rock? What have they already tried and failed?*

---

### The Bargain — Action (Core Mechanic)

Players can speak a wish aloud on Devil's Rock. The devil hears. The devil answers.

| Tier | GM Gains | Wish Scope | Examples |
|------|----------|------------|----------|
| **Minor** | 3 Fear | Small advantage | 3rd short rest today, specific information, a rare material |
| **Major** | 5 Fear | Significant boon | Return to Village, an ally appears, a threat removed, a location appears on the Path |
| **Desperate** | 7 Fear | The impossible | Bring back the dead, destroy a distant enemy, change history, destroy a location, leave the mist |

**The Warning:**

Before any bargain is struck, the GM must clearly state:

> *"This is a devil's deal. You will get what you ask for, but not how you want it."*

For **Desperate** tier bargains, the GM should pause and confirm with the table:

> *"This will change things permanently. Everyone okay with where this might go?"*

**GM Guidance:**

Desperate wishes should never fully succeed without massive consequence. "Bring back the dead" might return them changed, corrupted, or owing a debt of their own. "Leave the mist" might strand the party in a place worse than the mist. The monkey paw grants the letter of the wish, never the spirit.

*GM Question: What does the devil's voice sound like to each character—a stranger, a loved one, or their own voice reflected back?*

---

### The Storm's Echo — Action (Once Per PC Per Visit)

Unnatural lightning cracks across the sky with impossible frequency. Thunder rolls with whispers beneath it—fragments of bargains struck long ago, voices of the desperate who climbed before you. Those who listen carefully may learn from others' mistakes.

**Mechanics:**
- **Action:** Listen to the wind (costs 1 Stress, **once per PC per visit**)
- **Roll:** Instinct vs Difficulty 14
- **Success:** Roll d6 to determine which echo you hear
- **Failure:** Disturbing fragments that offer no useful warning—half-formed screams, sobbing, words in languages you don't know

**Past Bargains (roll d6):**

| d6 | The Wish | What They Got |
|----|----------|---------------|
| **1** | *"I wish my daughter would never leave me."* | She never did. She couldn't. Her legs fused to the floor of their home. She lived for decades, fed by her father, never able to step outside. He heard her crying every night. |
| **2** | *"I wish to hear my husband's voice one more time."* | She heard it. She still hears it. Every moment, every second—his last words, the ones he gasped as he died, repeating endlessly in her ears. She cannot hear anything else. Not music, not birdsong, not her children calling her name. Just his final rattling breath, forever. |
| **3** | *"I wish I could live forever."* | He does. He watched his children age and die. His grandchildren. The kingdom he knew crumbled to dust. His body continues—but it still ages. He is ancient now, blind, deaf, bones like paper, unable to move or speak. But his heart still beats. It will beat until the sun dies. |
| **4** | *"I wish my son would come home from the war."* | He came home. They all did. Every soldier from that battlefield walked home—though half of them had been buried for weeks. Her son stood at her door, still wearing the wounds that killed him, asking why she was screaming. |
| **5** | *"I wish my enemies would fear me."* | They did. Everyone did. His wife. His children. His allies. The fear in their eyes never faded, not even when he wept and begged them to see him as he was. He died alone, surrounded by people too terrified to hold his hand. |
| **6** | **The King's Deal:** *"Let our legacy be eternal, our riches never spent, our light never extinguished."* | The echo shows you a star falling from the heavens—beautiful, radiant, wrong. It descends in silence, trailing light that burns the color of amber and sickness. You feel the impact in your chest before the vision ends. Somewhere below the mist, a kingdom learned what "eternal riches" truly meant. |

*GM Question: Does a PC recognize their own deepest desire reflected in one of these bargains?*

---

### Speak — GM Tool (Reaction)

The devil does not wait patiently. When it senses desperation—or simply grows impatient—it extends an invitation that cannot be refused.

**Mechanics:**
- **Trigger:** GM spends 1 Fear
- **Effect:** One PC of the GM's choice is pulled onto the Throne Stone by an invisible force
- They still pay the **2 Hope cost** (the stone doesn't care how you arrived)
- No resistance possible

*GM Question: Why did the devil choose THIS character—what desire does it sense in them?*

## Feature Questions

- What happened to the king after his second bargain in the depths below? Does any part of him remain here on the plateau where it all began?
- Have others found Devil's Rock since the Starfall Kingdom fell? Are there bones at the bottom of the cliff—those who made wishes and immediately regretted them?
- Does the devil have a physical form, or is it only voice and force? Can it be bargained with in ways beyond the wish structure?
- What would happen if someone wished for the devil's destruction, or for the plateau itself to crumble?
- Is the connection between Devil's Rock and the Starfall Kingdom below still intact? Could a wish here affect the crystallized dead in the kingdom?

---

## Connection to The Starfall Kingdom

Devil's Rock is the Lightning Plateau referenced in Starfall Kingdom lore—the place where the desperate king climbed alone and called his wish into the storm. The meteorite that fell in answer to his bargain created the crystalline plague that destroyed his people.

The plateau still stands above the sea of mist, accessible via treacherous mountain paths. The devil that answered the king still listens. The storm still cracks with unnatural fury.

This is NOT where the meteorite landed—that impact site lies somewhere in the kingdom below. This is merely where the wish was spoken, where the devil heard, where the tragedy began.

*The Starfall Kingdom waits below the mist. Devil's Rock waits above it. Between them, countless crystallized dead dream of the star that made them eternal.*
