# HOLLOW WOODS

***Tier 1 Exploration***
*Ancient trees with hollow interiors large enough to shelter entire groups, their bark scarred with old carvings and forgotten histories.*
**Impulses:** Shelter weary travelers, preserve ancient knowledge, reveal traces of those who came before

> **Difficulty:** 11
> **Modifiers:** Unstable
> **Potential Adversaries:** Forest Beasts (Dire Wolf, Glass Snake), Rootbound Hermits

## FEATURES

***Ancient Hollows - Passive:*** Massive trees with hollow interiors large enough to shelter entire groups stand throughout this location, their bark scarred with ancient carvings depicting the world before the mist. The first time PCs visit this location, they can study these carvings and gain 3 Hope to distribute among themselves as they learn fragments of lost history and forgotten warnings.

  *What scenes do the ancient carvings depict? What civilization left these marks? What warnings or wisdom did they preserve for future travelers?*

***Carve History - Action:*** PCs can spend time carving a unique story, symbol, or message into the interior bark of a hollow tree. If the GM approves the carving as sufficiently unique and meaningful, the PC may spend 1 Hope to imbue it with lasting power. When PCs (in this session or future sessions) read a carved story, they can collectively retrieve 2 Hope from their respective carving to distribute among the party as they wish. Each individual carving can only grant Hope once - after being read and claimed, it becomes just another piece of the hollow's history.

  *What story does the PC carve? What moment from their journey do they immortalize? What wisdom or warning do they leave for future travelers? How does the group divide the Hope when they discover another's tale?*

***Hidden Ritual Circle - Counter System + Action:*** Behind dense root curtains lies an ancient ritual circle carved into the hollow's floor, surrounded by candle stubs, ash, and dried herbs. The circle only reveals itself to those who have walked these woods multiple times - **Start a Familiarity counter at 0. Each time PCs visit this location, increase the counter by 1. At 3: The ritual circle becomes visible to the party.** This counter persists across all sessions.

Once discovered, PCs can perform blood sacrifice rituals at the circle:

**Cleanse Corruption (Action):** A PC can offer their own vitality to purge mist corruption. For each HP sacrificed, remove 1 Mist token from any PC. PCs can use this ritual multiple times per visit, sacrificing as much HP as they choose.

**Sanctuary Blessing (Action):** The party can collectively offer 5 total HP (distributed however they choose) to invoke the circle's protective power. The forest spirits bless this location - it becomes Stable and protected from mist corruption until the next Path is created. The location maintains its grid position, cannot be made Mist-Touched by GM Fear spending, and resting here does not grant Mist tokens. This blessing persists even as the location moves between Path configurations, but ends when a new Path is generated. Can only be invoked once per visit.

  *What ancient forest cult created this circle? What spirits accept the blood offering? Why does the circle only reveal itself to those who prove their commitment by returning?*

***Bark Shedding Trees - Passive:*** The hollow trees periodically shed their outer bark in massive sheets, carpeting the ground with curled bark slabs that crunch and crack underfoot. The noise and unstable footing makes quiet movement nearly impossible - **PCs have disadvantage on Agility rolls to reposition themselves to Far or Very Far range, and disadvantage on all Stealth rolls while in this location.** However, the shed bark is useful and valuable: PCs can harvest sheets of bark for shelter material, crafting components, or fire fuel. When burnt, the bark emits a sweet and pleasant scent that fills the air. The bark is particularly prized by the Rootbound faction, who use it in their rituals and may trade favorably for quality specimens.

  *Why do the trees shed so aggressively? Is this natural growth or a defensive response? What grows beneath the freshly shed bark? How long does shed bark remain before decomposing?*

***Mushroom Ring Magic - Passive:*** When a PC steps into the fairy ring, they can choose to teleport to another mushroom ring on the current Path configuration (if one exists). The sudden displacement through fey magic is disorienting - the PC takes 2 Stress. If no other ring exists on the current Path, nothing happens and the magic lies dormant.

  *Where does the ring connect to this session? What strange sensations accompany the teleportation? What fey entities might notice frequent use?*
