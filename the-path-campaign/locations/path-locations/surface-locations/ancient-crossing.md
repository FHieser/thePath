# ANCIENT CROSSING

***Tier 1 Traversal***
*A crumbling stone bridge spans a deep chasm, guarded by broken monuments and treacherous stairs descending into darkness.*
**Impulses:** Test courage and balance, preserve forgotten history, warn of dangers below

> **Difficulty:** 11
> **Modifiers:** Unstable, Cave Mouth
> **Potential Adversaries:** Stone Guardians, Cliff Dwellers, Ancient Spirits

## FEATURES

***Crumbling Bridge - Counter System:*** The ancient stone bridge deteriorates with each crossing. Start a Bridge Stability counter at 7. Each time PCs cross the bridge or when the GM spends Fear during bridge encounters, reduce the counter by 1. At 4: Structural cracks widen - loose stones shift dangerously underfoot. The Loose Stone feature (below) now costs 0 Fear to activate. At 2: Extreme instability - every PC who crosses the bridge must mark a Stress as they carefully navigate the treacherous, crumbling footing. At 0: Complete collapse - the bridge crumbles entirely and can no longer be crossed, forcing all future travel through the underground passages.

  *How old is this bridge? What civilization built it? What caused such extensive damage over time?*

***Shattered Monument - Passive:*** The broken statue holds fragments of an important inscription. PCs who spend time studying the pieces can make a Lore roll. On a success, learn a general description of one of the nearby environments currently connected to this crossing. On a success with Hope, gain specific mechanical knowledge about a passive, action, or reaction feature in one of the nearby environments.

  *Who was honored by this monument? What caused it to be destroyed? What crucial information did the inscription originally contain?*

***Descent into Darkness - Action:*** The crumbling stone steps are treacherous to navigate in either direction. When PCs attempt to ascend or descend the stairs, they make an Agility action roll. On a success: Navigate the stairs safely without incident. On a success with Hope: You help the next person going up or down these stairs - they gain advantage on their roll. On a failure: Gain a Stress as you almost fall but catch yourself at the last moment. On a failure with Fear: Gain a Stress and take 1d8+3 physical damage as loose stones give way and you tumble hard down several steps.

  *What lies at the bottom of these ancient stairs? Why were they built leading down from the bridge? What has made its home in the depths?*

***Loose Stone Trap - Action:*** Spend 1 Fear to have a stone become loose under a PC's foot as they move across the bridge. They must make an Agility Reaction Roll. On a failure, they are Restrained until they succeed on a Strength and they must mark a Stress.

  *Which stones are most unstable? How deep are the gaps where stones have fallen? What dangers lurk in the crevices below the bridge?*


## FEATURE QUESTIONS

- What ancient kingdom or civilization built this impressive bridge?
- Why was a monument placed at this specific crossing point?
- What dangers lurk in the depths that the broken stairs once safely accessed?
- How might the bridge be stabilized or repaired for future travelers?
