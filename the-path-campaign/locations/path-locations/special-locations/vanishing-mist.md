# VANISHING MIST

**_Special Hazard Location_**
_An impenetrable wall of swirling mist that appears between destinations, disorienting travelers and separating the unwary from their companions. Those who enter become lost in endless gray, their voices fading into silence._
**Impulses:** Disorient travelers, separate groups, claim the lost

> **Difficulty:** —
> **Modifiers:** Unstable

## SPECIAL MECHANIC

This location does not appear on the grid in the normal way. When the Death XIII card is drawn during travel (whether through Random Draw placement or off-Path travel), players automatically visit this location between their previous position and their intended destination. There is no way to avoid or circumvent this encounter.

**Lost in the Mist:** The character who was leading the group or navigating is immediately marked **Lost in the Mist** and removed from the active player roster. This character vanishes into the endless gray, their form dissolving into swirling tendrils of mist. The remaining party members witness their companion fade away, unable to follow or intervene.

The lost character is not necessarily dead—they exist in a liminal state, trapped somewhere within the mist's embrace. The player must create a new character to continue play.

_The mist takes what it desires. No resistance. No farewell. Only silence and the endless gray._

## GM NOTES

**Narrative Recovery Hooks (Optional):** While the mechanical effect is permanent removal from the active roster, GMs may choose to introduce story elements that could lead to a lost character's eventual return:

- A rescue quest that requires traveling deep into particularly dangerous Mist-Touched territories
- Discovery of ancient rituals or Sacred Flame powers that can pull someone back from the mist's grasp
- Rare items or faction rewards that grant passage into the liminal space where lost souls drift
- A character's return as a changed being—perhaps partially corrupted, with fragmented memories, or bearing knowledge of what lies within the mist

These recovery opportunities should be rare, difficult, and carry their own costs. The threat of permanent loss must remain real for the mechanic to maintain its weight.

## FEATURE QUESTIONS

- What do lost characters experience while trapped in the mist?
- Are all lost souls in the same space, or does the mist fragment them into separate prisons?
- What would a character who returned from the mist remember? What would they have forgotten?
- Could the mist be controlled or bargained with by someone who understood its nature?
- What happens to characters who remain lost forever—do they eventually dissolve completely, or do they transform into something else?
