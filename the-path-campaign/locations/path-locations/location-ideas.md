# Initial Path - Location Ideas

*Collection of location concepts for the first Path configuration from Village in the Mist*

## Location Development Methodology

### **Step 1: Generate Individual Cool Locations**
- Brainstorm standalone location concepts with evocative names and one-line descriptions
- Focus on atmospheric, memorable places that spark imagination
- Don't worry about categories or themes yet - just collect interesting ideas

### **Step 2: Group Locations into Pairs or Thirds**
- Look for natural thematic connections between individual locations
- Create groups of 2-3 locations that work well together
- Consider spatial relationships (how locations connect or relate to each other)
- Name each group with a thematic title

### **Step 3: Iterate to Reach 3+ Subthemes per Location**
- Go through each group systematically
- Add third locations to pairs where needed
- Ensure each group has at least 3 distinct subthemes/locations
- Maintain thematic coherence while adding variety

### **Key Benefits of This Approach:**
- **Creative first**: Start with cool ideas before worrying about structure
- **Natural groupings**: Let themes emerge organically from the locations themselves
- **Systematic completion**: Ensure every location group is fully developed
- **Flexible iteration**: Easy to adjust and refine as you work through each group

### **Location Types:**
Currently developed: **Surface Locations** and **Underground Locations**

Future location types could include: Water/River Locations, Deep Forest Locations, Mountain Locations, Sea Locations, Swamp Locations, etc.

### Location Modifiers
*Optional overlays that transform base locations*

- **Stable**: Location maintains its relative grid position and connections between sessions. Cannot disappear into the Mist. May be naturally stable (ancient sites, Sacred Flame sanctuaries) or stabilized by players using Flame Essence rituals.
- **Unstable**: Location disappears into the Mist between sessions and may reappear randomly. Most new locations start as Unstable unless stabilized by players or inherently stable due to powerful anchors.
- **Underground**: Location connects only to other Underground locations or surface Cave Mouth locations. Creates separate underground navigation network.
- **Wild Beasts**: Location always contains an encounter with wild beasts - predators, territorial creatures, or dangerous wildlife.
- **Mist-Touched**: Location corrupted by prolonged mist exposure - adds Mist Walkers, reality distortions, and unstable terrain.
- **Inhabited**: Location has NPCs present - travelers, refugees, traders, or other communities.
- **Cave Mouth**: The entrance to a deep cave system carved into a rocky hillside - adds underground access to any location.
- **Water Feature**: Location includes flowing water elements (springs, streams, waterfalls, or pools) - adds aquatic travel options, fresh water sources, and potential water-based hazards or creatures.
