# The Rootbound

## Themes
- Shrewd guardians who protect their territories through calculated choice, not duty
- Ancient and mysterious - origins unknown, methods inscrutable
- Individual operators, each with their own sanctuary and agenda
- Master their domain completely, untouchable within their sphere of influence
- Neutral by choice, not compulsion - understand all sides and refuse them all

## History
- Some predate the imprisonment - ancient beyond reckoning, witnessed the god's crime
- Others arrived during the mist era, found natural sanctuaries and stayed
- Origins don't matter to them - druid, witch, ancient spirit, transformed mortal - only commitment to the sanctuary
- The natural places they guard seem to extend their lives (safety from mist? primal magic? both?)
- Connect through natural pathways: underground tunnels, mushroom circle teleportation, reading Path omens when sanctuaries are close
- No formal organization - just a loose network of individuals who occasionally communicate
- Age varies wildly: some centuries old, others merely decades, a few possibly millennia

## General Goals
- Protect their individual sanctuary from all threats (mist, flame, mortals, factions)
- Maintain the natural magic that keeps their domain safe
- Preserve absolute independence - never become tool or ally of any faction
- Pursue individual agendas unique to each Rootbound (varies widely)
- Test visitors to determine if they're threat, nuisance, or potentially useful

## Housing & Architecture
- Dwellings integrated into natural features (tree hollows, caves, living plants)
- Minimal construction - use what nature provides
- Each Rootbound's home reflects their personality and origin
- Hidden or camouflaged from casual observers
- Built to last centuries, adapting with the sanctuary itself

## Territories & Areas
- Natural sanctuaries - places that naturally resist mist without flame
- Each Rootbound claims one primary domain (varies from small groves to entire valleys)
- Found both on surface and underground wherever nature persists
- Scattered across the mist lands with no geographic pattern
- Connected by natural pathways: tunnels, mushroom rings, favorable Path omens

## Mist Protection
- Rely primarily on their sanctuary's natural mist resistance
- Each Rootbound has developed individual methods to handle brief mist exposure
- Methods vary wildly: herbal preparations, rituals, talismans, partial transformation, pure willpower
- Can venture into mist for limited time, but always return to sanctuary
- Deep understanding of WHY their location resists (knowledge other factions lack)
- Some enhance natural protection through maintenance and primal magic

## Subgroups
The Rootbound are highly individualistic - every sanctuary and guardian is different. No formal divisions, hierarchies, or shared practices beyond their commitment to protecting their chosen domain.

## Relationships with Other Factions

### Flame Aligned
**Fire Born**: Wary - Ancient beings recognize each other, but Fire Born's mission conflicts with Rootbound neutrality. Mutual respect, no cooperation.

**Flame Seekers**: Tolerated - Mortals are temporary visitors. Rootbound don't help or hinder unless they threaten the sanctuary.

### Neutral
**Ember Traders**: Transactional - Rootbound occasionally trade knowledge for supplies. Respect Traders' neutrality but don't trust them.

**Factionless**: Indifferent - Some Factionless seek sanctuary in natural places. Rootbound judge individually based on behavior.

**The Rootbound**: N/A (self)

### Mist Aligned
**Mist Clergy**: Dismissive - "Young ideology from young gods." Rootbound predate the conflict, don't engage with theological arguments.

**Mist Born**: Hostile when threatened - Mist Born can't easily enter natural sanctuaries, but Rootbound will destroy them if they attack the domain.

### Underground
**Deep Dwellers**: Neutral - Occasional contact through tunnel networks. Mutual understanding of "staying out of surface conflicts." Some Rootbound claim underground sanctuaries.

**Brass Mechanicorum**: Dislike - Ancient metal and artifice is an aversion to nature. Rootbound avoid mechanoid territories and distrust their presence.

## Rumors
*(What Flame Seekers and other mortals say about the Rootbound)*

- "There's a hermit in [natural sanctuary] who's been there longer than anyone can remember. Some say they don't age."
- "If you find a place where the mist just... stops, and it's not a flame sanctuary, don't go in. Something old lives there."
- "I once saw one of those step between the trees and disappear. Not into the mist - just gone. Like the forest swallowed them."
- "They say the natural sanctuaries are alive somehow. The Rootbound don't protect them - they ARE them."

## Item Identity
- **Theme:** Organic, grown or gathered items tied to natural sanctuaries. Effects tend toward **Restoration** and **Utility** - healing, sensing, navigating, and connecting to the natural world. Items feel living - wrapped in moss, carved from old wood, infused with sap. Each one is unique, reflecting the individual Rootbound who made it. They may respond to natural environments and go dormant in corrupted or artificial ones.
- **Typical Cost:** Narrative costs - the item demands something in return for nature (plant a seed, leave an offering, spend time tending a sanctuary). Some may require being in a natural sanctuary to recharge.

## Plot Hooks
- **The Mushroom Network Breaks**: Players discover mushroom circle teleportation between natural sanctuaries is failing. Rootbound are appearing confused in wrong locations, or not arriving at all. Something is severing their network - deliberate sabotage or natural decay?
- **Recruitment Offer**: A dying Rootbound approaches the party. Their sanctuary will lose protection when they pass. They offer to teach one PC how to become the new guardian - but it means staying there, potentially forever. Who chooses? What do they sacrifice?
- **The Vanished Warden**: A natural sanctuary's protection is failing. Its Rootbound guardian has disappeared. Investigation reveals why.
- **Test of Worth**: Players need passage through Rootbound territory. Must prove they won't harm the sanctuary to gain safe passage.
- **The Warden's Bargain**: A Rootbound offers players sanctuary during a mist storm - but demands payment afterward. Not gold, but a specific task: plant a seed in a dangerous location, retrieve an item from Brass Mechanicorum territory, or carry a message to another Rootbound through the mist.
