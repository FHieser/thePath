# Fire Born

## Themes
- Ancient servants transformed by their god's imprisonment
- Bound to flame as Mist Born are bound to mist
- Living paradox - sustained by the god they're trying to free
- Incomprehensible and archaic, relics of a bygone age
- Elusive as legends - most will never see one in their lifetime

## History
- Originally servants of the imprisoned god (already strange constructs even then)
- Refused to abandon their god when he was imprisoned
- The mist couldn't destroy them but couldn't tolerate them either
- Were transformed - forced into flame-dependency mirroring Mist Born's mist-dependency
- Now draw energy from their imprisoned god through sacred flames
- Millennia of this existence has made them increasingly alien and incomprehensible

## General Goals
- Free the imprisoned god by breaking his chains (their ancient purpose)
- Maintain sacred flames as wayfinding markers and route points along the Path
- Keep to themselves - millennia of mortal indifference bred mistrust
- Seek ancient sites and knowledge related to the imprisonment
- Spread flames for their own navigation, not mortal salvation

## Housing & Architecture
- No permanent structures - everything mobile
- Brazier carriers: elephant-sized to building-sized flame-bearing constructs
- Smallest unit: individual paladin on personal brazier carrier
- Largest: temple-constructs housing entire communities
- Ancient construction methods, some carriers thousands of years old
- Mobile life support systems, not just war machines

## Territories & Areas
- No fixed territory - travel the Path with their carriers
- Temporarily claim areas by parking temple-constructs
- Pilgrimage routes between suspected chain locations
- Sometimes create permanent sanctuaries by abandoning carriers
- Ancient ritual sites along the Path

## Mist Protection
- Sacred flames provide complete immunity while nearby
- Cannot leave their braziers without weakening/dying
- Project protective auras for others near their flames
- Cannot pursue enemies into mist (major tactical weakness)
- Dependent on fuel/magic to maintain flames

## Subgroups

### Bearers
- Maintain and transport the brazier carriers
- Fuel tenders, engineers, navigators
- Keep the flames alive and the constructs moving
- Essential support role

### Paladins
- Warriors who venture into the mist on missions
- Search for chain locations and ancient sites
- Most dangerous role - actively push into hostile territory
- Elite fighters with flame weapons

### Guardians
- Defend the massive temple-constructs
- Stationary protectors of communities
- Train and organize defenses against Mist Born
- Maintain perimeter security

## Relationships with Other Factions

### Flame Aligned
**Fire Born**: N/A (self)

**Flame Seekers**: Indifferent - Mortals benefit from Fire Born navigation flames, but Fire Born don't particularly care. Tolerated presence, not protected allies.

### Neutral
**Ember Traders**: Transactional - Fire Born trade for fuel and supplies. Purely practical relationship, no warmth or trust.

**Factionless**: Ignored - Unless they interfere with Fire Born goals or routes, beneath notice.

**The Rootbound**: Wary - Ancient beings recognize each other. Seldom cooperate, but mutual respect for age and power.

### Mist Aligned
**Mist Clergy**: Hostile - Ideological enemies, though Fire Born may not even understand their arguments clearly. Different ancient perspectives.

**Mist Born**: Hostile - Active combat. Mutual hatred, but also unwanted kinship (both bound to their respective elements by the mist).

### Underground
**Deep Dwellers**: Indifferent - Underground retreat irrelevant to Fire Born's ancient mission.

**Brass Mechanicorum**: Curious - Ancient mechanoids might remember the pre-imprisonment era. Potential sources of knowledge about chains.

## Rumors
*(What Flame Seekers and other mortals whisper about the Fire Born)*

- "My grandfather claimed he saw one once, seventy years ago. A massive carrier moving through the mist like a walking temple. Never saw it again."
- "The ancient flames that mark the Path? Some say those are Fire Born routes, not sanctuaries for us. We're just... incidental."
- "I heard a scholar tried to follow one of their carriers for weeks. When he returned, he couldn't speak our language anymore - only strange words that made the air shimmer."
- "There's a flame sanctuary near the Highland Watch that's been burning for centuries. No one tends it. Maybe they abandoned it... or maybe they're watching from somewhere we can't see."

## Item Identity
- **Theme:** Ancient flame-forged artifacts that empower through proximity to Sacred Flame. Effects tend toward **Disruption** and **Protection** - burning back threats or shielding the bearer from corruption. Items feel archaic and alien - inscribed with symbols no mortal language can parse, warm to the touch, faintly glowing. They grow weaker or inert the further you get from flame.
- **Typical Cost:** Requires proximity to Sacred Flame or consumes Flame Essence. Items may also generate Fear when used (the mist reacts to flame being weaponized).

## Plot Hooks
- **Reignition Witness**: A flame sanctuary's brazier is extinguished by the mist. Days later, a Fire Born Paladin emerges from the mist itself to reignite it - the first Fire Born sighting in the region in living memory.
- **The Walking City**: Traveling the Path, players witness a colossal temple-construct emerge from the mist - a city-sized carrier with towers and structures on its back, moving with impossible grace. It passes silently and vanishes back into the fog.
- **Dying Flame**: Players discover a Fire Born Paladin desperately fighting Mist Born, badly wounded. Their personal brazier carrier's flame is guttering, nearly extinguished. Do they help? Can they even communicate?
- **The Village Secret**: Research or exploration reveals the eternal flame at the heart of Village in the Mist isn't just a brazier - it's an ancient Fire Born temple-construct that was parked there centuries ago and never moved again.
