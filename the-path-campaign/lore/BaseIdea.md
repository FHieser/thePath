# High Lore

- There is a pantheon of gods
- One of the gods gave the mortals something against the will of the other gods
- He was then imprisoned based on that
- To make his prison secure they made the mist that surrounds his lands
- The mist makes traveling and orientation nearly impossible
- The mist also creates its own guardians (mist creatures)
- The imprisoned gods power didnt loose its influence completely 
- The power is manifesting in flames that can deny the mists influence

- The lands of the mist themselves can easily be reached from the outside, but escaping them is nearly impossible

### Overview
The Mist Born's history begins with the god's imprisonment and the Ancient Guardians appointed to watch over THE Nothing. Unlike other factions who evolved organically, the Mist Born's origins trace to both divine appointment (the Guardians) and mortal tragedy (those broken by the void). The faction guards THE Nothing because the Ancient Guardians—giant divine constructs still watching in the depths—taught them the catastrophe that awakening would bring.

### Timeline

**The Imprisonment Era:**
- The imprisoning gods wanted to banish War and Expansion from their realm
- They took primordial void—the Nothing that existed before creation—and used it as the chains
- Creating the world from nothing was the hardest act; using that same nothing as a prison was the solution
- Planted three void-spheres at the lowest depths to chain the god in unconscious sleep
- Took something from their divine realm and manifested it in the mortal world as the divine field (mist) to maintain his unconsciousness and contain THE Nothing
- **Created and appointed Ancient Guardians**—giant divine constructs (titanic crocodile, kraken, crab) to watch over THE Nothing and ensure the god remained asleep
- The imprisoning gods departed, viewing this realm as either the prison itself or a distant place beneath their concern
- The god's ancient servants (who would become Fire Born) refused to abandon him and were transformed

**The First Mortals - Broken by the Abyss:**
- The first mortals wandered into the mist and descended into the gigantic underwater caverns
- They found the Ancient Guardians—massive divine beasts standing eternal watch over THE Nothing
- **"If you gaze into the abyss, the abyss gazes back"**
- Mortals who looked upon THE Nothing were broken by it—minds shattered by encountering absolute void
- The Ancient Guardians took them in, recognizing they could not return to normal life
- Taught them to live with what they had witnessed
- Taught them the purpose: the god's domain is War and Expansion; awakening him means catastrophe for the world
- Taught them the full cosmological balance: Expansion vs Nothing, and why the god must sleep
- These broken mortals became the first mortal Mist Born

**The First Monasteries:**
- Broken mortals, under guidance of the Ancient Guardians, established underwater sanctuaries in the caverns descending toward THE Nothing
- Built diving bell meditation chambers and coral-encrusted temples
- Ancient Guardians taught doctrine: "To guard THE Nothing, one must descend into it"
- Mortals began deliberate fusion with the mist—accepting the transformation as part of their purpose
- Developed into a faction: not servants of imprisoning gods, but protectors of the world taught by divine appointees
- The Ancient Guardians remained unchanged—stoically performing their eternal task while mortals transformed around them

**The Living Organism Discovery:**
- Monks realized the divine field (mist) wasn't merely fog—it BREATHED
- The manifested piece of the divine realm was alive, a living organism in the mortal world
- Coral-void growths began appearing on those who descended deepest
- Initially feared as mutation, then reinterpreted as sacred transformation
- Ancient Guardians confirmed: fusion with the organism was part of the path for mortals
- "Enlightenment through fusion with the void" became core teaching
- The deeper mortals descended, the more they merged with the organism
- The Ancient Guardians themselves remained uncorrupted—their divine construction immune to transformation

**The Spawned Generation:**
- As the mist-organism grew (fed by THE Nothing's presence), it began budding new Mist Born
- These spawned constructs inherit fragmented memories from mortals consumed by the mist over millennia
- Spawned also inherit the Ancient Guardians' teachings through absorbed mortal memories
- Created strange, alien personalities blending many lives and Guardian knowledge into incomprehensible wholes
- Ancient Guardians and original monks recognized spawned as "younger siblings"—children of the organism
- Spawned possess fragmented understanding of the cosmological balance (Expansion vs Nothing) through inherited knowledge
- The Ancient Guardians continue teaching when mortals descend deep enough to find them

**The Expansion Crisis (Current Era):**
- THE Nothing's influence began fueling accelerating mist expansion
- With the god's domain of Expansion imprisoned, THE Nothing operates unbalanced
- Divine field growing in size and territory, consuming more of the mortal realm
- The Ancient Guardians continue their stoic watch, unchanged by millennia
- They know the cosmological balance—awakening the god would unleash War and Expansion to fight THE Nothing
- But this would mean divine conflict the mortal realm cannot survive
- The expansion accelerates, yet the Guardians remain at their posts
- The imprisoning gods do not return to address the crisis

**Relationship to Fire Born:**
- Both factions predate the imprisonment—Ancient Guardians and Fire Born both witnessed the god's chaining
- Fire Born were servants who refused to abandon their god and were transformed by mist's reaction to them
- Mist Born were mortals broken by THE Nothing and taught by Ancient Guardians
- Transformed by opposed elements and philosophies:
  - Fire Born: Flame/Peaks/Consciousness/Freedom (idealize god as liberator/fighter who will free them)
  - Mist Born: Void/Depths/Unconsciousness/Protection (taught by Guardians that awakening means catastrophe)
- Active combat driven by ideological conflict and ancient enmity
- Tragic recognition of unwanted kinship—both transformed beyond mortality, both devoted to their purpose
- Fire Born don't understand (or care) that freeing their god means unleashing War and divine conflict
- Mist Born (those who retain Guardian teachings) understand the full cosmological balance but believe preventing divine war is worth the cost of expanding void
- The Ancient Guardians and Fire Born's original forms may have been enemies even before the imprisonment

# Factions

There are different factions in the lands of the mist, that try to survive or even thrive there.

## Flame Aligned

### Fire Born
- Flame Paladins, direct followers of the imprisoned god
- Actively trying to free him by breaking his chains

### Flame Seekers
- General survivors seeking flame sanctuaries
- Just trying to get by and survive

## Neutral

### Ember Traders @todo rework
- Merchant-scholars who transport flames between sanctuaries
- Map the Path and sell route knowledge
- Forced neutrality - everyone needs them

### Factionless
- Everyone without faction alignment
- Range from peaceful homesteaders in natural sanctuaries to bandit crews (the Lost)
- Survive on their own terms

## Mist Aligned

### Mist Clergy
- Believe the imprisonment is divine justice and must be maintained
- Use persuasion and politics over violence
- Oppose the Fire Born ideologically

### Mist Born
- Creatures created by the mist itself
- Enforce the prison system, eliminate threats to the mist
- Target flame followers especially

## Underground (Outside the Spectrum)

### Deep Dwellers
- Underground communities where mist doesn't reach
- Thrive in the deepest layers

### Brass Mechanicorum
- Ancient machines and mechanoids from underground
- Old structures scattered everywhere
- Origins and purpose unclear

### The Rootbound
- Shrewd guardians of natural sanctuaries
- Highly individualistic, each with their own domain
- Neither flame nor underground - nature resists mist naturally
- Ancient and mysterious, neutral by choice
