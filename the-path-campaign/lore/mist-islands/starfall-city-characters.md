# The Starfall City — Characters

*The capital of the Starfall Kingdom is not just a place — it is a collection of human stories, frozen in crystal. These six characters are woven through the city's locations. Players can discover them piece by piece, never in a forced order. Each one is a different answer to the same question: what do you do when everything falls apart?*

---

## The Heroes

### The Nurse
**Core:** Love as presence. Gave everything. Died a saint.

The nurse and the soldier were lovers. When the plague came she begged him to stay. He refused and left. She lost herself in work — grieving for him while pouring that grief into every patient she could reach. She was not trying to be a hero. She was trying to survive the absence of the person she loved by making herself necessary to everyone else.

She found peace in it, eventually. Died surrounded by the people she had saved, in the Pest House or the Sanctuary Keep. Not alone. Never alone.

She never knew why he really left.

---

### The Mechanic
**Core:** Fear as creation. Helped from a distance. Made peace with it.

Always a loner — the plague didn't make him one, it just confirmed it. Too scared to face the horror directly, he built brass clockwork robots and sent them out to do what he couldn't. The people of the city knew *someone* was making them. They didn't know who.

When he felt the crystals beginning to take him, he didn't run. He finished the watermill — his greatest work, the thing that would keep the robots running indefinitely after he was gone. Then he sat down at the workbench, wrote a letter, and waited.

The robots are still running. Still cleaning the streets of a dead city, still carrying out their last instructions. Players who follow one will be led back to his workshop.

He is still at the bench. Crystallized, but upright. He chose not to leave.

**What's on the workbench:** His own farewell letter — an Abschiedsbrief. And beside it, in a small box found by one of his robots while cleaning the city, someone else's letter. Something in it touched him deeply. He built a brass mechanical bird to carry it to its destination. He died not knowing where that destination was.

*The two letters, the bird, and what connects them are the thread.*

## The Villains

### The Soldier
**Core:** Love turned to rage turned to guilt. A villain who thought he was a hero.

He loved the nurse. He was ordered by the guard captain, following the king's decree, to burn her parents' house — part of the effort to contain the plague. He did it. He followed orders.

He couldn't tell her. She would have broken down. So he carried it alone, and that secret weight curdled into rage at the upper caste, at the king, at the system that made him do it. He left her — she begged him to stay — and went to fight.

He fought his way to the castle. He sealed the king behind his door. His last words: *"Do not open this door again. This shall be his end."*

He caught the plague in the fight. Crystallized at the castle entrance. Still there. An eternal guardian — penance dressed as justice.

He never went back to find her. His injuries were too severe. He died not knowing she had forgiven him.

**Connection to gameplay:** The soldier's crystallized form seals the Lord's Castle. Players need the drill from the meteor location to get through. He is the reason the castle is inaccessible.

---

### The Merchant
**Core:** Took everything. Died alone on a mountain of gold.

He moved fast. When the meteorite fell, he understood before anyone else that the crystals were valuable — not as medicine or material, but as novelty, as beauty, as proof you had been close to something extraordinary. His gang harvested and sold them before the plague connection was understood. First money. Clean money, or so he told himself.

When the crystal trade was banned, he didn't miss a step. He had the networks. He changed the product. Medicine, food, passage out of the city — anything desperate people needed at any price the desperate would pay. Not cruel in the cartoonish sense. Practical. Transactional. He told himself he was providing a service.

When the quarantine decree threatened to close his operation, he didn't argue with authority. He sent a man to the scaffold square and had him throw a stone. One stone. The crowd did the rest. The riot destroyed official order, the market filled the vacuum, and the Merchant operated freely in the chaos he had manufactured.

As the plague worsened and resources thinned, the gang became a liability. He began removing loose ends. Members disappeared across the city — one district, then another. Each one knew something. None of them got to tell it.

He died alone, surrounded by everything he had accumulated. No one came. No one mourned him.

**Connection to the Mechanic:** The workshop sits adjacent to Craftsmen's Ward — the gang's distribution hub. The Mechanic watched the operation from his workshop windows, through cracks and keyholes. He saw the stone thrown. He saw the bodies disappear. He sent his robots into those streets to clean. He never intervened. He never told anyone. He just watched, and built, and sent the robots out again.

**Thread structure:** A scavenger hunt through the city — meeting places, evidence of the gang's work, then the bodies where the Merchant cleaned house. The vault is at the end. It can only be found by players who have seen everything.

### The Noble
**Core:** Grief turned to dissolution. Gave everything away. Faded forgotten.

An old patriarch. Had ruled long and well. His proudest legacy was the university — a place of knowledge, learning, care. His name on the gate. His belief that a city looks after its people, made permanent in stone.

When the plague came, the security chief quarantined the sick inside it. Then the king ordered it burned. The soldier carried out the order. The patriarch watched it burn — people screaming inside, doctors dying at their posts.

*The nurse's parents were among them. They were doctors working in the quarantine zone when the fire was lit.*

His last image: on his knees before the inferno. The same fire the soldier watched from the other side.

He understood then that things don't protect people. His university hadn't protected anyone. So he gave everything else away — wealth, the manor, every resource — distributed to whoever needed it. Not out of heroism. Out of having nothing left to hold onto. A good deed done without will to live.

He faded. No monument. No grave. Forgotten.

**The web he sits at the center of:**
- He built the university → it drew the nurse's parents there as doctors
- The security chief quarantined it → the king ordered it burned → the soldier lit the fire
- The patriarch watched it burn → so did the soldier → neither chose it → both were destroyed by it
- The nurse spent her last days doing what her parents taught her, never knowing they died in a fire her lover lit

---

### The Security Chief
**Core:** Fear turned to control turned to nothing. Became the Warden.

Where the mechanic let fear drive him to *create*, the security chief let fear drive him to *control*. He couldn't even sign the first quarantine decree — the Noble took the quill from his hand. He read it publicly at Scaffold Square, got a stone to the head, came away bleeding. At the Riot Market a crystal zombie sprayed shards across his hands. He felt the dust on his skin and knew.

He gave the order to burn the university. Pointed at the soldier. Pointed at the Sealed District. Two gestures that destroyed everything.

He climbed the Council Hall tower alone as the city burned below him — the university, the market, all of it. He breathed out and saw crystals in the air. Then he fell.

He didn't die from the fall. He transformed. Dozens of crystallized dead fused to him as he lay at the base of the tower. He rose as the Warden — a massive accumulated crystal entity, the Security Chief buried somewhere inside it.

The Warden still haunts the Council Hall district. Players who complete his story thread understand what they are truly fighting.

**Connection to gameplay:** The Warden is the city's roaming threat — the Wake system's ultimate consequence. Players who defeat him see The Warden's Last Memory: flashes of consciousness from the fall to the transformation to the moment he first saw the players coming.

## The Love Story

The nurse and the soldier are the emotional spine of the city.

- He burned her parents' house on orders. Couldn't tell her.
- She begged him to stay. He left anyway.
- She grieved. Found peace in her work. Died surrounded by others.
- He sealed the king. Crystallized at the door. Died regretting it.
- She left him a note: *"I understand. I forgive you. Come back when it's done."*
- One of the mechanic's robots found the note. The mechanic built the brass bird to deliver it. He never programmed the destination.
- The soldier never received it.

## City-Wide Notes

**Tone:** Mounting tension (every step risks awakening crystallized dead) with humanity underneath — players should feel both the horror and the grief.

**Structure:** Web — no forced order. Players discover these stories as they explore.

**The Lord's Castle:** Sealed. Inaccessible until players obtain the drill from the meteor location. The soldier guards the door.

**The Mechanic's Robots:** Still active. Still cleaning. Follow one back to the workshop to find the brass bird and the letter.

**Three Emotional Notes the City Should Land:**
1. The cost of rage — the soldier
2. The grace of presence — the nurse
3. The tragedy of distance — the mechanic
