# The Starfall Kingdom

*A thematic "island in the sea of mist"—a cluster of interconnected locations telling the tragic story of a civilization destroyed by a devil's bargain, a falling star, and the crystalline plague that turned eternal riches into eternal death.*

---

## The Devil's Bargains

### The First Bargain - Lightning Plateau

High upon a windswept plateau where lightning crashes with unnatural frequency, a desperate king sought salvation for his struggling kingdom. The site was rumored to grant wishes for those willing to pay the price. The king climbed alone to the summit and called out his desire into the storm.

*"Let our legacy be eternal, our riches never spent, our light never extinguished."*

The devil heard. The devil answered.

From the heavens fell a star—a meteorite of corrupted celestial material from realms beyond mortal comprehension. It crashed into the kingdom's heartland, bringing with it the fulfillment of the king's wish. The meteorite's substance was heavenly in origin but twisted by the devil's touch, crystalline and radiant with inner light.

Within days, the plague began.

### The Second Bargain - The Deepest Chamber

As his people's lungs transformed into glowing crystal shards, as the symphony of cracking glass echoed through every home, the king realized the horror of his wish. The "eternal riches" were not gold or prosperity—they were his people, crystallizing from within, suffocating slowly as translucent fragments grew in their chests.

In desperation, he descended to the deepest chamber beneath his castle—a place of power, of ancient stones and forgotten purpose. There he called upon the devil once more, willing to pay any price to stop what he had begun.

*"Stop the spread. I'll pay anything."*

The devil agreed. The plague froze at the kingdom's borders, its advance halted. The crystallized dead ceased their final gasps and fell silent. The price was the king's life—though whether he died, transformed, or endures in some twisted state within that deepest chamber, none can say. The castle's lowest level remains sealed, its secrets lost to time and mist.

But the devil's bargain carried an unintended consequence, a quirk of physics the devil neither planned nor cared about: the corrupted heavenly crystals respond to divine energies. When touched by the sacred flame—the imprisoned god's power manifest as war and conquest—the crystallized dead awaken. They rise as warriors, answering an ancient call to battle that burns within the flame itself.

The king bought containment at the cost of creating a kingdom of sleeping soldiers, waiting for fire to rally them once more.

## The Crystalline Plague

### Nature of the Affliction

The plague does not spread through touch or air in the traditional sense. It is the meteorite's corruption taking root, a transformation that begins in the lungs and spreads outward. Victims feel tightness in their chest first—a pressure that builds over days. Then comes the crystallization.

Lung tissue hardens into translucent shards that glow with faint inner light. Each breath becomes torture. The sound of breathing transforms into a delicate, horrible symphony—the tinkling crack of glass breaking, over and over, with every labored inhalation. Victims can see the glow through their skin as darkness falls, their chests lit from within like lanterns of bone and crystal.

Death comes slowly, through suffocation. The crystal grows until no air can pass. The victim's final breath creates one last crystalline note, and then silence.

The bodies remain, chest cavities filled with glowing gemstones of compressed flesh and divine corruption. Valuable beyond measure. Horrific beyond words.

### The Meteorite

The meteorite was no natural celestial object. It came from the devil's domain—corrupted heavenly material, substance that exists in realms beyond the mortal world. Its crystalline properties resonate naturally with divine energies, reacting to the fundamental forces that shape reality.

This was not a deliberate trap. The devil granted the king's wish with twisted literalism, caring nothing for the consequences. The fact that these crystals respond to Sacred Flame and divine mist is simply how heavenly materials function when corrupted and brought into the mortal realm. Nobody planned it. Nobody controlled it. It simply *is*.

The meteorite's current location remains a mystery—whether it still exists intact at its impact site, whether it shattered and scattered, or whether the king's second bargain somehow consumed or transformed it is unknown. Some locations within the lordship may hold fragments, pieces of the fallen star embedded in stone or soil, still radiating their terrible gift.

## Divine Energy Interactions

### Sacred Flame Awakening

The corrupted heavenly crystals embedded in the plague victims' bodies resonate with divine energies. When Sacred Flame—the imprisoned god's power manifest—comes near these crystallized dead, the resonance creates a horrifying effect.

The imprisoned god's domain is War, Expansion, Conquest. His power burns with the essence of action, of movement, of battle. When his flame touches the crystal-plague victims, it acts as a rally call. *Rise. Fight. Conquer.*

The crystallized dead respond. Eyes that have been dark for decades flare with inner light. Limbs that have been still begin to move. The cracking glass symphony begins again—not with breathing, but with motion, with awakening, with the terrible animation of warriors called to battle.

They rise to fight. They attack anything living. They are not mindless—they retain fragments of who they were, enough to wield weapons, to coordinate, to hunt. But they are driven by the flame's call to war, by the divine imperative to expand and conquer that saturates the imprisoned god's power.

This is not the devil's design. It is emergent consequence—the physics of divine energy meeting corrupted heavenly material. The flame calls for war. The crystals answer.

### Mist Dormancy

The divine mist—power from the imprisoning gods' realm—has the opposite effect. Where Sacred Flame is action and war, the mist is stillness and unconsciousness. It is the power that maintains the imprisoned god's sleep, that enforces dormancy and quiet.

When mist touches the crystallized victims, stillness reinforces stillness. The divine field of unconsciousness keeps them frozen, inactive, safe in dreamless sleep. The dormant dead remain dormant.

This creates a cruel irony: in the plague-ravaged kingdom, the mist is mercy. Travelers shrouded in divine fog can move through streets of crystallized corpses without fear of awakening them. The very force that corrupts and threatens in the wider world here acts as protector, maintaining the terrible peace of a kingdom frozen in time.

### Player Implications

This divine energy interaction creates tactical consequences that players must recognize and navigate:

**In normal mist-corrupted areas:**
- Sacred Flame = salvation (cleanses corruption, provides safe sanctuaries)
- Mist = threat (corrupts those who linger, spawns mist adversaries)

**In The Starfall Kingdom:**
- Sacred Flame = doom (awakens crystallized undead warriors)
- Mist = safety (maintains dormancy, prevents awakening)
- Harvesting crystals = awakening (disturbing the bodies triggers animation)

Players must learn to identify plague corruption versus mist corruption. The strategies that save them elsewhere will doom them here. Flame Essence—their most precious resource for cleansing mist corruption—becomes a liability. Using it to rest, to heal, to protect themselves will instead summon an army of crystal-animated warriors.

**The Harvesting Dilemma:** Players attempting to extract the valuable glowing crystals from corpses face a terrible risk. The act of harvesting—cutting into crystallized lungs, prying out gemstone shards—disturbs the dormant dead. Even without flame present, the violation of their crystallized bodies triggers awakening. The corpse being harvested animates first, followed by those nearby. The encounter begins immediately, with players caught mid-extraction, tools in hand rather than weapons. The temptation of unimaginable wealth must be weighed against combat while unprepared.

There are no Flame-Touched safe sanctuaries here. Fire brings only battle. Greed brings the same.

## The Lordship's Fall

### Timeline

**The Prosperous Era:**
The kingdom was not always cursed. Once it thrived—a lordship of reasonable size and wealth, with a capital city of several districts, outlying villages, and defensive walls marking its borders. The king ruled from his castle, justice was administered, trade flourished. It was unremarkable in its normalcy.

**The First Bargain:**
Driven by ambition or desperation (accounts vary), the king climbed the Lightning Plateau and made his wish. The meteorite fell. Within days, the first victim began coughing crystal.

**The Spread:**
The plague moved through the capital like wildfire. Districts were sealed, barricaded from both inside and out as authorities tried desperately to contain the infection. Quarantine villages were established for the afflicted. Mass graves were dug. Physicians experimented with increasingly desperate cures. Nothing worked.

The walls that had once defended against external threats now turned inward—guards preventing refugees from entering, keeping the plague contained within the lordship's borders. Families gathered at the edges, hoping for entry that never came. Inside the walls, order collapsed. Justice became mob rule. Temples became final stands against both plague and panic.

**The Second Bargain:**
The king descended to the deepest chamber and paid the ultimate price. The plague froze. Victims mid-breath simply stopped, crystallized in place. The dead stopped dying. The spread halted at the lordship's borders.

**The Mist's Embrace:**
With the plague contained but not cleansed, with thousands of crystallized corpses throughout the kingdom, the mist slowly consumed the land. It crept in over months, then years, until the entire lordship existed within its embrace. Survivors who had fled spread word of the cursed kingdom. The surrounding territories considered it a blessing—the plague was contained, the horror hidden, the kingdom lost to the fog.

Now The Starfall Kingdom exists as an island in the sea of mist, frozen in its moment of tragedy, waiting.

### The Capital City

The capital was the heart of the lordship—a city of stone and timber, multiple districts serving different purposes. Market squares for trade. Temple quarters for worship. Residential districts rising in tiers. The castle overlooking all from its elevated position.

When the plague reached its peak, the city became a maze of barricades. Some districts were sealed by authorities attempting quarantine. Others were barricaded by survivors attempting protection. The Sealed District has scratches on the inside of every door—desperate attempts to escape that never succeeded.

The city's current state is a frozen moment of horror. Aristocrats crystallized mid-dance in the palace ballroom. Accused prisoners still chained to benches in abandoned courtrooms. Weapon racks stripped bare, walls marked where desperate hands grabbed anything sharp. Market stalls overturned, looted, destroyed when order finally collapsed.

Parts of the city remain accessible to those brave or foolish enough to enter. The mist shrouds everything, maintaining an eerie quiet. The crystallized dead wait in their dormancy, countless glowing forms visible through windows and in doorways. Until flame comes to wake them.

## Locations Within The Starfall Kingdom

### The Giant Walls

The lordship's defensive walls were meant to keep threats out. In the plague's final days, they served a darker purpose—containing the infection, preventing the cursed from fleeing to spread crystalline death beyond the kingdom's borders.

- **Border Post** - The checkpoint where guards turned back their own people. Families pleaded to be let through. Children begged for entry. The guards held their posts, tears streaming, weapons raised. Eventually the guards crystallized too, still standing at their station, translucent hands gripping translucent spears.

- **Message Tower** - The rookery where messenger birds were housed and trained. The birds remain, generations later—descendants of those original ravens and pigeons. They nest in the silent tower, flying between the mist-shrouded locations of the dead kingdom. They don't flee the plague zones. They were born here. This is their home.

- **Breach Point** - A collapsed section of wall where siege engines rot amid scattered bones. In the plague's final days, those outside tried to break in—whether to rescue loved ones or loot the dying kingdom, none survived to say. The siege engines themselves are crafted from crystallized corpses, weapons of bone and glowing crystal that still gleam faintly in the mist.

- **Refugee's Hope** - The camp at the lordship's edge where families waited for entry permission that never came. Tents still stand, preserved by the mist's timeless embrace. Crystallized forms huddle together—parents shielding children, lovers holding each other, all frozen in their final moments of hope. On clear nights, their chests glow like a field of lanterns.

### Lost Villages

Beyond the capital, outlying communities fell to the plague with less ceremony and more horror.

- **Plague Warren** - An abandoned quarantine village where doors are nailed shut from the outside. Authority's final attempt at containment. Something still scratches from within some of the houses—whether crystallized undead trying to escape or vermin that have made nests among the corpses, none have investigated closely enough to determine. The scratching never stops.

- **The Charnel Pit** - A mass grave dug when individual burials became impossible. The pit exhales cold breath that rises as visible vapor even in summer. Carrion birds circle endlessly overhead but never land—they sense something wrong. The countless bodies at the bottom glow like a constellation viewed from above, a terrible mirror of the stars that sent the plague.

- **Ashen Sepulcher** - A collapsed tomb half-buried in volcanic ash (the lordship's northern border touched volcanic highlands). The sepulcher's passages lead far deeper than the structure's exterior suggests—impossible architecture carved before the plague, perhaps used during it for purposes unknown. Those who enter report hearing whispers in languages predating the lordship, and seeing shadows that don't match any light source.

### Sealed City District

The capital's central quarters were barricaded during the plague's height—a desperate attempt to protect the uninfected that failed catastrophically.

- **Sealed District** - A city quarter barricaded from both sides. The inside barricades suggest those within knew they were infected and chose containment. The outside barricades show the healthy refused to let them out. Scratches mar every door from the inside. The glowing corpses behind those doors number in the hundreds.

- **Pest House** - The hospital where the dying were sent when hope of cure still existed. Multi-story building of stone and suffering. Each floor represents a different stage of plague progression—first floor for early symptoms, second floor for advanced cases, third floor for the crystallizing. The fourth floor was reserved for those already transformed but still alive, kept for study. The physicians crystallized on the fourth floor, surrounded by their subjects.

- **Physician's Folly** - A doctor's manor filled with desperate experimental cures and preserved specimens. Jars line the walls containing crystallized organs suspended in formaldehyde—lungs in various stages of transformation, carefully labeled and dated. Surgical tools rest on tables mid-procedure. Notes in increasingly frantic handwriting document failed cure attempts. The physician's own crystallized form slumps in his study, quill still in hand, final notes unfinished.

- **The Burning Fields** - Scorched land where they tried to burn the sickness away. The ash is still warm to the touch, even after decades. No explanation exists for this phenomenon—some property of the crystalline plague makes the burn eternal. The ground crunches underfoot with fragments of fused crystal and charred bone. At night, embers still glow beneath the ash.

### Last Stand

When order collapsed, these locations witnessed the kingdom's final desperate hours.

- **Scaffold Square** - The execution ground where justice became mob rule in the final days. Gallows still stand with crystallized forms hanging from nooses—some executed for spreading the plague, others for stealing food, others for crimes as simple as causing panic. The mob that did the hanging crystallized too, frozen mid-cheer, mid-rage, mid-terror.

- **Sanctuary Keep** - A temple where survivors made their last stand against plague and panic. The doors were barred from inside. The crystallized dead within number nearly a hundred—families, clergy, soldiers, all seeking divine protection that never came. They died praying. Their forms still kneel before empty altars, hands clasped, chests glowing.

- **Riot Market** - The trading plaza destroyed when order collapsed and looting began. Stalls overturned, goods scattered and trampled. Crystallized forms locked in eternal struggle—merchants protecting their wares, looters grabbing what they could, guards trying to restore order. All frozen mid-conflict. The market square looks like a battlefield of glass statues.

### The Old Castle

The seat of power, the king's domain, the site of both triumph and tragedy.

- **Lord's Observatory** - The tower where the last lord watched his domain die through expensive telescopes. The instruments still point at specific locations throughout the kingdom—the Pest House, the Burning Fields, Scaffold Square. A log book records observations in the lord's hand, documenting the plague's spread with detached clinical precision until the final entry: "It has reached the castle."

- **Scribes' Archive** - The library documenting the lordship's final days in increasingly frantic handwriting. Early records are neat, formal, bureaucratic. Later entries become desperate—crossed-out sections, tear stains smudging ink, barely legible scrawls. The final volume sits open on a desk, last entry reading: "The king has descended. May the gods forgive what he does in the depths."

- **The Last Ball** - The palace ballroom where aristocrats danced while the world burned outside. Historical accounts suggest they knew the end was coming and chose beauty over survival. Crystallized nobles in fine dress still maintain dance positions—couples frozen mid-waltz, musicians at their instruments, servants carrying empty platters. Chandelier crystals and body crystals reflect light in eerie harmony.

- **Armory Hollow** - The castle's weapon vault, stripped bare in the final days. Empty racks line the walls. Marks on the stone show where desperate hands grabbed anything sharp—even decorative pieces unsuited for combat. A few crystallized guards remain, having refused to abandon their posts even when their comrades fled with the weapons they were meant to protect.

- **Empty Courthouse** - The halls of justice abandoned mid-trial. The accused still chained to benches, their crystallized forms slumped in shackles. The judge's seat empty—they fled rather than deliver verdict. Court records scattered on the floor document increasingly harsh sentences for plague-related crimes, culminating in execution orders for anyone showing symptoms.

- **Forgotten Oubliette** - Secret dungeon cells below the dungeon, accessible only through a concealed passage. Prisoners who were left to starve in darkness when the plague began. Their crystallized forms show they died slowly—scratch marks on walls, bones scattered from cannibalism attempts, final positions of bodies huddled in corners. None knew why they were forgotten. The plague never reached them here. They died of abandonment.

- **The Deepest Chamber** - The lowest point beneath the castle, a place of ancient stone and unknown purpose. The stairs descend far beyond the castle's visible foundation. The entrance is sealed, though whether by the king's final act, by the plague's power, or by later hands is unclear. What remains within—the king's fate, the bargain's aftermath, the truth of what price was paid—is left to mystery and speculation.

### Lightning Plateau

High above the lordship, accessible by treacherous mountain paths, the plateau where the first bargain was struck waits in storm-wracked isolation.

Lightning crashes here with unnatural frequency, even on clear days. The ground is scorched and charred, marked with patterns that might be ritual circles or might simply be where lightning repeatedly strikes. Rumors claim the plateau grants wishes to those desperate enough to climb it and call out their desires. Whether this was always true, or whether the devil's presence made it so, none can say.

The king came here alone. What he saw, what he heard, what convinced him to make his wish—these details died with him. The plateau offers no answers, only the crash of thunder and the smell of ozone. Some claim to hear whispers in the lightning's roar. Others report seeing shadows that move independently of any light source.

This is NOT where the meteorite landed. That impact site lies elsewhere, perhaps within the capital, perhaps in the surrounding lands. The plateau is merely where the wish was spoken, where the devil heard, where the tragedy began.

### Rootbound Refuge

Amid the crystalline horror, one location stands apart—a grove where the plague never reached, where nature's power held corruption at bay.

A Rootbound guardian maintains this sanctuary, ancient and mysterious. Whether they arrived after the plague to protect what remained pure, or whether their presence prevented the corruption from spreading here in the first place, is unclear. They do not explain themselves to visitors.

The grove is not Flame-Touched—no Sacred Flame burns here. It is not Stable in the traditional sense—it exists within the mist like all the lordship's locations. But it is *clean*. The trees grow healthy. The ground is soft with moss and fallen leaves. No crystallized corpses litter the earth. The air smells of living things rather than glass and death.

The Rootbound guardian tolerates visitors but does not welcome them. They see this place as evidence of nature's resilience, proof that not all corruption can spread forever. They witness to mortal greed and divine consequences, maintaining their vigil in silence.

This may be the only true safe haven within The Starfall Kingdom—a place where neither flame's awakening nor plague's horror can reach.

## Current State

### The Mist's Embrace

After the king's second bargain froze the plague at the lordship's borders, the mist crept in. Slowly at first—just tendrils along the edges. Then thicker, deeper, until the entire kingdom existed within its shroud.

The plague stopped spreading beyond the borders as bargained. The lordship became an island—isolated, contained, lost. For the surrounding territories, this was a blessing. The cursed kingdom was sealed away, the crystalline horror hidden in fog, the threat neutralized through divine abandonment.

The Starfall Kingdom now exists in temporal suspension. The mist preserves everything—buildings don't crumble, bodies don't decay, even the ash in the Burning Fields remains warm. Time passes differently here, or perhaps doesn't pass at all. The kingdom waits, frozen between life and death, between curse and mercy, between wake and sleep.

Until someone brings flame.

### Rumors and Reputation

*(What travelers and Flame Seekers whisper about The Starfall Kingdom)*

- "They say there's a cursed lordship in the deep mist where the dead glow like lanterns. Every corpse has gemstones where their lungs used to be. Fortune beyond measure, if you can stomach harvesting them."

- "My grandmother remembered The Starfall Kingdom before it vanished. Said the mist swallowing it was the gods' mercy—better lost than spreading that glass-lung death to the whole world."

- "Met a treasure hunter once who claimed he'd been there. Showed me a crystal that glowed with inner light. Said it came from a corpse's chest. Refused to tell me more, just kept staring at the crystal and muttering 'don't bring fire, don't bring fire.'"

- "The king wished for eternal riches and got his wish. His whole kingdom turned to living gemstones. Careful what you ask for when the devils are listening."

## Faction Perspectives

**Fire Born**: View the kingdom as proof of their god's power—Sacred Flame awakens the dead to battle, demonstrating the War domain's strength. Some see it as a sacred site where flame-crystal resonance could be studied or harnessed.

**Mist Born**: See mortal foolishness meeting inevitable consequence. The mist's consumption was correct—corruption contained, spread prevented, divine balance maintained. May guard borders to keep explorers out.

**Flame Seekers**: Terror and temptation in equal measure. Stories speak of unimaginable crystal riches, but also of treasure hunters with flame lanterns who were never seen again. Must choose between flame safety and awakening the dead.

**Rootbound**: The guardian in the grove witnesses mortal greed meet divine consequence. Other Rootbound view it as evidence nature can resist even corrupted heavenly materials. May know truths about the bargains they won't share.

**Ember Traders**: Avoid when possible—flame is their livelihood, and a place where flame awakens the dead threatens their entire operation. Some might attempt flameless crystal harvesting for the wealth, likely ending badly.

**Factionless**: Opportunistic interest from treasure-seekers to desperate refugees. The Lost see it as ultimate high-risk looting—enter without flame, harvest crystals, leave before awakening. Whether expeditions succeed or add more corpses is unknown.

**Mist Clergy**: Use it as morality tale proving divine justice and consequences of hubris. The mist's consumption represents imprisoning gods' judgment. Oppose Fire Born interest, seeing flame-awakening as further abomination.

**Deep Dwellers**: Likely unaware—it's a surface phenomenon. If underground passages connect to Forgotten Oubliette or Deepest Chamber, they might encounter plague from below with unknown consequences.

**Brass Mechanicorum**: May have records from before the plague. Unknown if constructs are vulnerable to crystalline infection or immune as machines. Could explore without infection fear, though flame-awakening remains threat.

## Plot Hooks

- **The Crystal Harvest**: An Ember Trader approaches the party with an unusual commission—they need someone to enter The Starfall Kingdom and harvest crystalline plague gems from corpses. The trader has buyers willing to pay fortunes for these "starfall crystals," believing them to have magical properties. The trader knows flame awakens the dead, so they need adventurers who can work in silence, in mist, without relying on Sacred Flame protection. Can the party resist using flame even as Mist tokens accumulate? What happens if they accidentally wake even one crystallized warrior in close quarters?

- **The Sealed Archive**: A Fire Born scholar believes the Scribes' Archive in The Starfall Kingdom's castle contains records of the devil's bargain—specific wording, ritual details, perhaps even the devil's true name. They hire the party to retrieve specific volumes. But the Archive sits deep in the castle, surrounded by crystallized nobles and guards. The Fire Born scholar insists on accompanying the party, carrying a Sacred Flame lantern "for safety." Will the party allow this, knowing what flame does here? Can they convince the scholar to leave the flame behind?

- **The Rootbound's Request**: The Rootbound guardian maintaining the grove within The Starfall Kingdom approaches the party with an unusual problem. Something is disturbing the grove's boundaries—creatures, or travelers, or something else is attempting to breach the sanctuary. The Rootbound asks for help investigating and eliminating the threat. But the grove is surrounded by the plague kingdom on all sides. Reaching it requires crossing through areas thick with crystallized dead. And the Rootbound refuses to explain what they're protecting within the grove, or why maintaining it matters so much.

- **The King's Final Testament**: Rumors surface of a sealed chamber beneath The Starfall Kingdom's castle containing the truth of the devil's bargain—what price the king truly paid, what he learned, perhaps even a method to reverse or contain the plague permanently. Multiple factions want this information: Fire Born seeking to understand flame-awakening, Mist Clergy seeking proof of divine justice, treasure hunters seeking the king's personal vault. The party must navigate competing interests while descending to the Deepest Chamber, where something unknown waits in sealed darkness.

- **Awakening the Army**: A radical Fire Born faction believes The Starfall Kingdom represents the ultimate weapon—thousands of crystallized warriors that awaken when exposed to Sacred Flame. They plan to deliberately wake the entire kingdom and somehow direct this army against the mist itself, or against enemies of their imprisoned god. They need the party's help establishing flame sources throughout the kingdom's key locations simultaneously. Do the party aid this potentially catastrophic plan? Can they sabotage it from within? What happens if they succeed and truly wake the entire kingdom at once?

- **The Plague Spreads**: Despite the king's second bargain supposedly containing the plague at the lordship's borders, cases of crystalline lung transformation begin appearing in distant settlements. Impossibly, the plague is somehow spreading again. The party is hired to trace the infection back to its source—a trail that leads to The Starfall Kingdom and suggests someone is deliberately removing and transporting infected material. But why? Who would weaponize this horror? And can the party stop them before the plague escapes containment entirely?
