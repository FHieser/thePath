# The Path — Campaign Pitch

*A Daggerheart campaign frame for 3–5 players.*

---

## The World

An otherworldly Mist has swallowed the world. Maps are useless. Landmarks vanish between visits. The landscape itself shifts.

The only reliable way to travel is **The Path** — a network of ancient, interconnected locations linked by walkways that persist through the fog. Sacred Flames burn at key points, holding the Mist at bay. Between those flames: anything can happen.

Your players live in **The Village in the Mist** — a sanctuary built around one of those ancient flames. Each session they venture out onto The Path, navigate its dangers, and return changed. The world remembers what they do there.

---

## What Makes This Campaign Different

### The Path Shifts

Between sessions, the GM reconfigures The Path. Some locations persist — Stable, permanent, safe to plan around. Others are Unstable and may be gone when players return. New locations appear. Old ones drift away into the fog.

Players draw from a tarot deck to generate each journey's route. The cards create the structure. Their choices fill it.

### Locations Remember

Every location tracks player visits, significant choices, and NPC relationships. Players who return to a clearing they burned will find ash. An NPC they helped will remember. A location they left in ruin stays ruined.

This isn't a reset-between-sessions dungeon crawl. It's a world that accumulates.

### Low Prep, High Replayability

The campaign frame is built around modular location cards — 14 locations (9 surface, 5 underground) plus a Village hub with 5 distinct areas. Each location has its own character, encounter structure, and modifier system.

A GM can run a one-shot by drawing three cards. A long campaign grows the same map session by session into something the players shaped themselves.

## The Setting

### The Mist

Not weather. Not natural. The Mist is the prison of a god — an ancient divine being sealed away before memory, with the Mist itself as the bars of his cage. The Sacred Flames that keep it back? Wayfinding markers left by the **Fire Born**, ancient servants of that god who have spent millennia searching for the chains that bind him.

Players don't start knowing any of this. They start knowing: the Mist is dangerous, flame is safety, and the Path is the only way through.

### The Sky

Above the Mist: **twenty-five planets**, each governing a domain. The Red One — Danger. The Pale One — Hope. The Twin — Choice. The Shrouded One — Mist. The Burning One — Flame.

Travelers who know the planets' names learn to ask the right questions of the sky. Travelers who don't still notice that The Red One is pulsing tonight.

### The Factions

The world fractured along ideological lines when the Mist came. Key factions operating across The Path:

- **Fire Born** — Ancient constructs bound to Sacred Flame, alien and incomprehensible after millennia of isolation. They maintain the Path's flames for their own purposes. Mortals benefit incidentally.
- **Rootbound** — Something older than the Mist. They were here before, and they are still here, patient in the slow way of ancient things.
- **Mist Born** — Creatures that have adapted to the Mist. Enemies of the Fire Born. Potential mirrors for what the players could become.
- **Flame Seekers, Ember Traders, Mist Clergy, Deep Dwellers, Brass Mechanicorum** — and more, each with their own angle on survival.

## The Campaign Arc

### Tier 1 — Path Walkers
Players are new arrivals to the Village. They learn to navigate The Path, survive Mist encounters, and begin piecing together the world's history. Scope: 3–5 connected locations, local mysteries, the first glimpse that something much larger is at stake.

### Tier 2 — Path Shapers
Players begin influencing Path configurations, engaging with faction politics, and uncovering pre-Mist ruins. The world starts responding to their reputation.

### Tier 3 — Path Guardians
Players are protecting the network itself — multiple Villages, region-wide systems, and the slow revelation of the Mist's true origin. The imprisoned god becomes real.

## A Location: Starfall City

Late in the campaign, The Path leads somewhere it shouldn't be able to go: **a city**.

The Starfall Kingdom's capital fell to a crystal plague decades ago. The Mist rolled in as the last survivors died. By all rights the city should be gone — consumed, shifted, scattered like everything else that doesn't persist.

Instead it's here. Intact. Because the Mist that corrupts everywhere else is the only thing holding the crystal together.

The city is a 3×3 grid of nine districts. Players enter at the gates and push inward toward the Lord's Castle — sealed, at the top, beyond the grid. The crystallized dead are everywhere. They sleep when undisturbed. The wrong noise, the wrong light, the wrong harvested fragment wakes them.

**The Wake System** tracks each district's disturbance level. Wake 0 is safe. Wake 3 brings the Warden — a mass of fused crystallized bodies that moves with terrible purpose and does not respect district boundaries.

The city has stories to tell. **Crystal wind chimes** form naturally in doorways where significant moments left impressions — decades of compressed memory crystallized into sound. Touch one and play it. The city has six story threads running through it: a soldier, a nurse, a mechanic, a noble, a merchant, a security chief. Their stories intersect. The security chief's thread ends with him becoming the Warden.

Players who explore everything learn what happened. Players who explore nothing still have to survive.

## Why Daggerheart

The Path is built on Daggerheart's core systems without modification. **Hope and Fear** map naturally onto the campaign's rhythms — the Path is where Fear is earned, the Village is where Hope is spent. The **Duality Dice** create the uncertainty that makes each location visit feel genuinely dangerous without requiring adversary bloat.

The **asymmetrical design** (PC mechanics vs. adversary mechanics) means location hazards can be built to feel alien without requiring players to track monster statblocks. The Mist is a GM tool, not a ruleset players have to memorize.

## In Play

> *"You draw the cards. Three locations between the Village and the Ancient Crossing. The first two you've seen before — the Windswept Grasslands in winter, the Trading Grounds where you left that fire burning. The third card is face-down. You don't know what's there until you arrive."*

> *"The chime in the doorway rings as you enter the pub. The Nurse is last through the door, slightly apart from her friends. She looks back once at the university towers — still lit, still standing. Then follows them inside. You remember: at 1,3, the university is ash."*

Players experience a world that is simultaneously improvised and accumulated — procedurally generated but permanently marked by everything they've done in it.
