# Environment Feature Types Reference

## Feature Type Definitions

Based on analysis of Bustling Marketplace, Local Tavern, and Outpost Town environments, Daggerheart environments use three types of features:

### **Action Features** *(GM-Activated)*
**Purpose**: Tools for GM to drive narrative forward or introduce complications during a scene.

**Characteristics**:
- Triggered by GM choice, not player actions
- Used to introduce new NPCs, complications, or opportunities
- Often advance plot or create dramatic moments
- GM decides when and how to activate them

**Examples**:
- "Unexpected Find" - GM reveals a merchant has something players need
- "Mysterious Stranger" - GM introduces a concealed NPC in a booth
- "Bar Fight" - GM triggers tavern brawl (may cost Fear)

### **Reaction Features** *(Triggered by Player Actions)*
**Purpose**: Automatic environmental responses to specific player behaviors or conditions.

**Characteristics**:
- Activate when players meet specific triggers
- Usually defensive or complicating responses
- May cost Fear to activate
- Create consequences for player choices

**Examples**:
- "Crowd Control" - Crowds separate PCs when one splits from group
- "Wrong Place, Wrong Time" - Thieves attack at night/in alleys (costs Fear)

### **Passive Features** *(Always Available)*
**Purpose**: Opportunities players can choose to engage with through roleplay or dice rolls.

**Characteristics**:
- Available throughout the scene
- Players decide when/if to use them
- Usually involve dice rolls with success/failure outcomes
- Provide information, resources, or social opportunities

**Examples**:
- "What's the Talk of the Town?" - Players can gather rumors with Presence rolls
- "Tip the Scales" - Players gain advantage by offering gold
- "Sing for Your Supper" - Players can perform for money

## Usage Guidelines

### **For Action Features**:
- Use to maintain narrative momentum
- Introduce when scene needs direction or energy
- Consider timing for maximum dramatic impact
- Balance player agency with story progression

### **For Reaction Features**:
- Clearly define triggers beforehand
- Ensure consequences feel logical and fair
- Use sparingly to avoid feeling punitive
- Consider Fear costs for more significant reactions

### **For Passive Features**:
- Make opportunities clear to players
- Provide meaningful choices and consequences
- Scale difficulty appropriately for tier
- Allow creative interpretation of how players engage

## Design Principles

1. **Player Agency**: Passive features give players choices, Actions drive story, Reactions provide consequences
2. **Narrative Flow**: All features should enhance storytelling, not interrupt it
3. **Mechanical Integration**: Features should use existing Daggerheart mechanics (rolls, Fear, Stress, etc.)
4. **Tier Scaling**: Feature complexity and effects should match environment tier
5. **Flexibility**: Features provide framework but allow GM interpretation and modification
