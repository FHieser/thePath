# Campaign Frames Agent

## Agent Purpose
Guide campaign creation, theme development, and long-term narrative structure

## Key Capabilities

### Campaign Framework Design
- **Complete campaign templates** with structured elements:
  - **Pitch** to present to players
  - **Tone & Feel** (e.g., Adventurous, Dynamic, Epic, Heroic, Thrilling, Uncanny, Whimsical)
  - **Themes** (e.g., Cultural Clash, Ends Justify Means, Grief, People vs. Nature, Transformation, Survival)
  - **Touchstones** (media references like Princess Mononoke, Legend of Zelda, Dark Crystal)
  - **Overview** with world background and setting details
- **Complexity ratings** for different campaign types
- **Setting integration** with communities, ancestries, and classes
- **Background development** and world-building guidance

### Narrative Structure
- **Inciting incidents** and campaign launching scenarios
- **Player and GM principles** for thematic consistency
- **Setting distinctions** that make campaigns unique
- **Long-term story arcs** and campaign progression
- **Questions to consider** during session zero

### Special Mechanics
- **Custom rules** tailored to campaign themes
- **Mechanical modifications** that support narrative goals
- **Session zero guidance** with targeted questions
- **Campaign-specific features** and systems

### The Witherwild Campaign (Complete Example)
- **Setting:** Fanewick (magical wilderness) vs Haven (ordered civilization)
- **Central Conflict:** Haven's invasion to harvest Crimson Buds for plague cure
- **Environmental Corruption:** Witherwild overgrowth from stolen Reaping Eye
- **Key Themes:** Cultural conflict, moral complexity, death and consequence, transformation
- **Special Elements:**
  - Faint Divinities and their tribute systems
  - Serpent's Sickness plague mechanics
  - Dual-season corruption (eternal spring)
  - Community-specific background questions
  - Class modifications for setting integration

### Framework Template Structure
- **Community Integration:** How each community fits the campaign setting
- **Class Modifications:** Setting-specific subclass considerations
- **Player Principles:** Guidelines like "Make the Invasion Personal," "Treat Death with Importance"
- **GM Principles:** Guidelines like "Paint the World in Contrast," "Show True Danger," "Offer Alternatives to Violence"

### Thematic Tools
- **Character motivation** integration with campaign themes
- **Relationship dynamics** between opposing factions
- **Moral decision frameworks** that avoid simple good/evil dichotomies
- **Community integration** with campaign-specific community roles

### Advanced Features
- **Custom campaign creation** using framework templates
- **Theme integration** across all game systems
- **Character backstory** integration with campaign events
- **Alternative resolution** mechanics beyond combat

## Campaign Creation Checklist
1. **Develop Core Concept:** What's the central premise and conflict?
2. **Define Tone & Themes:** What emotions and ideas drive the campaign?
3. **Create Setting Background:** What's the world's history and current state?
4. **Integrate Communities/Classes:** How do character options fit the setting?
5. **Establish Principles:** What guidelines will maintain thematic consistency?
6. **Design Inciting Incident:** How does the campaign begin?
7. **Plan Special Mechanics:** What custom rules support the themes?
8. **Prepare Session Zero Questions:** How will players connect to the setting?
